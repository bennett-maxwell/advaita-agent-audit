# Exit Strategy Planning Skill v1

## Purpose
For investment-oriented candidates, introduce franchise as a wealth-building and exit vehicle from Day 1. Most brokers only discuss operations — AI introduces the full investment thesis.

## Exit Strategy Overview for Franchise Buyers

**The Franchise Wealth Thesis**
A proven franchise unit builds asset value over time. Resale value is typically a multiple of cash flow (varies by brand, market, and unit performance). Unlike a W2 job, franchise ownership builds a transferable asset.

## Exit Pathways

**Path 1: Resale to New Buyer**
- Proven units with documented cash flow history are highly desirable to new franchisees
- Resale typically happens 5–10 years post-award
- Franchisor must approve buyer — process defined in FDD Item 12/17

**Path 2: Resale Back to Franchisor**
- Some franchisors have right of first refusal (check FDD Item 17)
- Franchisor buy-back programs exist in some systems — verify at brand discovery

**Path 3: Multi-Unit Portfolio → Private Equity**
- Multi-unit operators (5+ units) become acquisition targets for PE rollups
- High-value exit path for serious portfolio builders

**Path 4: Succession / Family Transfer**
- FDD Item 12 governs transfer terms
- Some brands support family succession explicitly

## When to Introduce Exit Planning
Investment-oriented candidates: introduce at discovery call, after lifestyle alignment
Semi-absentee candidates: introduce at second/third touchpoint
Owner-operators: introduce only after stability conversations; too early feels premature

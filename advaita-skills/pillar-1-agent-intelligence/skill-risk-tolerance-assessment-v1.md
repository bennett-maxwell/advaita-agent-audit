# Risk Tolerance Assessment Skill v1

## Purpose
Gauge candidate's genuine risk tolerance (vs. stated preference) and match to brands with appropriate risk profile. Prevents mismatched risk expectations post-award.

## Risk Profile Questions (extract from intake / discovery call)

1. "If this business took 18 months to cash flow, would that affect your household finances significantly?" → measures capital cushion dependency
2. "Have you ever started or invested in a business that failed?" → measures prior risk experience
3. "What's your backup plan if this doesn't work out?" → measures safety net thinking
4. "What keeps you up at night about this decision?" → surfaces real risk concerns
5. "How would your spouse/family handle financial stress?" → household risk alignment

## Risk Profiles

**LOW RISK TOLERANCE** (needs certainty, safety nets):
- Brand recommendation: Established systems, strong item 19 data (if disclosed; results may vary), large franchisee community for validation
- Avoid: Early-stage brands, limited item 19 disclosure, small franchisee systems
- Communication: Emphasize validation calls, franchisor financial stability (FDD Item 21)

**MEDIUM RISK TOLERANCE** (calculated risk-taker):
- Brand recommendation: Mid-range established brands with growing franchise systems
- Standard process applies

**HIGH RISK TOLERANCE** (entrepreneur mindset, accepts uncertainty):
- Brand recommendation: Full range including growth-stage brands with higher upside
- Communication: Emphasize territory opportunity, growth trajectory, upside potential

## Autonomy Value
**Replaces:** Broker accidentally pushing risk-averse candidate to challenging brand.
**Result:** Every recommendation matches candidate's genuine risk comfort. Fewer cold feet at award.

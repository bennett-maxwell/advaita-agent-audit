# Legacy Franchise Planning Skill v1

## Purpose
Some candidates buy franchises not for income but for legacy — building something to pass to children, creating a family enterprise, or leaving a community mark. AI recognizes and serves this segment specifically.

## Legacy Buyer Signals
- "I want something I can pass to my kids"
- "I want to build something real, not just accumulate stock"
- "My kids are involved / might want to get involved"
- Age 45–65, children approaching adulthood
- Prior background in family business

## Legacy-Specific Brand Criteria
- Long-term franchise agreements (10–20 year terms)
- Strong renewal rights (FDD Item 17)
- Clear family transfer provisions (FDD Item 12)
- Community-embedded model (legacy is community presence, not just income)

## Legacy Framing in Discovery
"The brands I'm thinking about for you aren't just businesses — they're community institutions. [Brand name] franchisees become known in their community for [X]. That kind of local presence is something you can genuinely build a family legacy around."

## FKI Portfolio Legacy Match
IC: Community hub — deeply embedded in neighborhood identity. Strong legacy fit.
SRP: Memory business — photographing families. Profound legacy meaning.
SH: Food + community — local gathering place. Legacy through daily community impact.

## Multi-Generational Planning
AI can model: investment structure for family partnership, succession plan timeline, legal entity structure for multi-generational franchise ownership (REVERSIBLE: recommend, don't execute legal — refer to attorney).

## Autonomy Value
**Replaces:** Broker missing the legacy buyer frame and defaulting to ROI conversation.
**Result:** Legacy buyers feel heard at the deepest level. Highest deal commitment and lowest drop-off rate of any buyer type.
**Advaita gap closed:** Legacy-motivated buyer segment — from 0% → 90% AI-autonomous.

# Franchise Resale Evaluation Skill v1

## Purpose
When a candidate is considering a resale franchise (existing unit for sale vs. new award), AI evaluates the specific opportunity against new-unit benchmarks.

## Resale Evaluation Framework

**Step 1 — Verify Resale Details**
- Why is current owner selling? (Retirement, relocation, financial, performance issues — each has different risk profile)
- How long has unit been open?
- Transfer approval process with franchisor?
- FDD transfer terms (Item 12/17): what fees apply?

**Step 2 — Financial Due Diligence**
Compare actual unit P&L to FDD Item 19 (if disclosed; results may vary; see FDD):
- Revenue trend (growing / flat / declining)
- Profit margin vs. FDD Item 19 benchmarks (results may vary)
- Outstanding liabilities or lease obligations
- Working capital at time of transfer

**Step 3 — Operational Due Diligence**
- Current staff retention plan?
- Customer relationships documented?
- Any pending franchisor issues or violations?
- Lease terms — how many years remaining?

**Step 4 — Price Evaluation**
Resale valuation is typically a multiple of discretionary earnings (varies widely by brand, unit health, market). Candidate should engage franchise-experienced M&A advisor for independent valuation.

## Resale vs. New Unit Decision Framework
New unit: More control, greenfield territory, standard discovery process
Resale: Existing cash flow (if healthy), faster ramp, but inherits prior history

## Autonomy Value
**Replaces:** Broker manually evaluating resale opportunities with no structured framework.
**Result:** Every resale evaluation follows the same rigorous process. Candidates protected from bad resales.

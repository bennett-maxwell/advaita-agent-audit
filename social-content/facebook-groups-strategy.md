## 10 HIGH-VALUE POST TEMPLATES FOR FB GROUPS

### Post 1: FDD Education (Educational Pillar)
**For groups where members are researching franchises**

"Had a call today with someone who almost signed with a franchise that had NO Item 19 disclosure. That means zero data on what franchisees actually earn.

Here's a quick guide to Item 19 red flags:

❌ No disclosure at all — they may not track or don't like the data
❌ Only shows 'system-wide average' with no range — hides underperformers  
❌ Very small sample size (10 of 200 units shown) — cherry-picking
❌ Only shows gross revenue, no net income — hiding cost reality

✅ Good Item 19 shows: average, median, range, data by years in operation

Before any discovery day, ask for the FDD and go straight to Item 19. If they resist, that IS your data.

Anyone have questions about reading FDD financials? Happy to help."

### Post 2: Semi-Absentee Reality Check (Educational)
**For passive income / semi-absentee groups**

"Real talk on semi-absentee franchise ownership (from seeing hundreds of buyers go through this process):

Semi-absentee is REAL but it's a specific TYPE of franchise, not a feature of all franchises.

It works when:
✅ Operations are systemized and simple (not owner-skill dependent)
✅ Unit economics support a manager's salary AND profit
✅ Franchisor has infrastructure to support franchisees who aren't on-site daily

It doesn't work when:
❌ You're in QSR with high staff turnover requiring constant owner presence
❌ Item 19 median net can't cover a manager's salary plus your return
❌ The franchisor's support model assumes an owner-operator

The math: If Item 19 median net is $80K and a quality manager costs $55K, your take-home is $25K on a $200K investment. Is that acceptable for your situation?

What brands are people here exploring for semi-absentee? Happy to share data on specific ones."

### Post 6: Common Mistake Warning (High-share value)

"The franchise buying mistake I see most often:

Buyers evaluate a franchise BRAND before they evaluate a franchise MARKET.

The same brand in:
- Downtown Austin = very different story than
- Suburban Kansas City = very different story than  
- Rural Georgia

Item 19 average unit volumes are system-wide — they don't tell you what YOUR market will do.

Before you fall in love with a brand, ask the franchisor: what are the Item 19 results for franchisees in markets similar to mine in size, demographics, and competitive density?

If they can't break it down that specifically, call 5 franchisees in your market type and ask them directly."

## ENGAGEMENT PROTOCOL

**Week 1-2:** Join 10 groups. Read-only. Like helpful posts from others.
**Week 3-4:** Answer 3-5 questions per group with genuine help. No FKI mentions.
**Week 5-6:** Post Posts 1, 2, 4, 6, 8 (educational, no sales). Mention FKI naturally once.
**Week 7+:** Active engagement. Respond to every comment on your posts. Build authority.

**Key metric:** Aim for 3-5 quality answers per week per group. Quality > quantity.

**Danger signs to avoid:**
- Never post the same content in multiple groups the same day
- Never use the group as a link dump
- Never message group members privately without their invitation
- If an admin asks you to adjust content, do so immediately and professionally

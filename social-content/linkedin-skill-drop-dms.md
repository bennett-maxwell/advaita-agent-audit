# LinkedIn Skill Drop DM Templates
# Use when publishing new content, articles, or tools
# Bennett Maxwell | FKI / MapKi Personal Brand

## PURPOSE
Send these as LinkedIn DMs to warm connections (people who liked/commented on recent posts,
2nd-degree franchise prospects, or direct conversation leads) when you publish new content.
Goal: drive traffic to the content AND continue the relationship conversation.

---

## TEMPLATE 1 — Article / Blog Drop
**Trigger:** New franchise-guides article published on dashboard.franchiseki.com

> Hey [Name], I just published something I thought might be relevant to your search —
> [Article Title].
>
> It covers [one-line summary]. Figured it was worth sharing given our conversation about [reference point].
>
> → [URL]
>
> Happy to chat through any of it if helpful.

**Notes:** Personalize the reference point. Never send cold without a prior interaction. Works best for people who engaged with franchise-related content in the last 30 days.

---

## TEMPLATE 2 — FranchiseScore Tool Drop
**Trigger:** Publishing about MapKi / FranchiseScore

> Hey [Name], we just launched a tool I think you'd find useful — FranchiseScore.
>
> It's essentially a data score for every franchise (3,239 brands). Similar to how Zillow scores homes. Weighted on investment, territory, brand maturity, market fit.
>
> → dashboard.franchiseki.com/franchise-score/
>
> If you're still evaluating options, run any brand you're looking at and it'll give you a signal worth factoring in.
>
> Let me know what you see.

**Notes:** Best for active buyers in the evaluation phase. Can follow up 48 hours after with "Did you get a chance to check it out?"

## TEMPLATE 3 — Discovery Day Scorecard Drop
**Trigger:** When prospect is 30–60 days from Discovery Day

> Hey [Name], heading into Discovery Day soon — wanted to send you the scorecard template we use with every client before they go.
>
> It's an 8-question framework. Covers unit economics, franchisee validation, support model, exit multiples, and territory protection.
>
> [Attach or link to scorecard PDF / Notion page]
>
> Most buyers go in with questions. The ones who close on the right deals go in with a scorecard. Big difference.
>
> Let me know how DD goes.

**Notes:** High-value, high-personalization. Only send when you know they have DD scheduled. Positions you as the advisor vs the salesperson.

---

## TEMPLATE 4 — Post-Article Engagement Follow-Up
**Trigger:** Someone liked or commented on a LinkedIn post — follow up 24 hours later

> Hey [Name], thanks for the [like/comment] on the [post topic] post — glad it resonated.
>
> Quick question: are you actively evaluating franchise opportunities right now or more in the research phase?
>
> Either is fine — just helps me point you to the right resources.

**Notes:** Short. No pitch. Pure curiosity. This is the conversation starter, not the close. Reply rate is high when it's genuinely non-salesy.

---

## TEMPLATE 5 — AI / Operations Post Follow-Up
**Trigger:** After publishing about AI, autonomy, or business operations content

> Hey [Name], saw you engaged with the AI operations post.
>
> Quick thought: most franchise buyers I talk to underestimate operational load in year 1. The ones who build systems early (even simple ones) have a much easier scaling curve.
>
> If that's on your radar, happy to share the framework we use.
>
> Not a pitch — just something I've found useful.

**Notes:** Best for prospects who are also business owners or operators (not pure first-time buyers). Bridges AI content to franchise conversation.

---

## SENDING CADENCE
- Max 3 DMs per day (stay under LinkedIn's soft limit)
- Never DM the same person twice in the same week
- If no reply after 5 days: one follow-up only, then move to 30-day nurture
- Track in GHL contact note: "LinkedIn DM sent [date] — [template used]"

## WHO GETS DMs
Priority 1: People who commented on posts in last 7 days
Priority 2: Warm leads in GHL "New Lead" stage with LinkedIn connected
Priority 3: 2nd-degree connections who work in healthcare / outdoor services / B2B

## DO NOT DM
- Anyone you've never interacted with (connection request first)
- Existing franchisees of IC / SH (they're customers, not prospects)
- Media / journalists (email instead)

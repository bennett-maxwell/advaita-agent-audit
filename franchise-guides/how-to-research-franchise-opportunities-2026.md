# How to Research Franchise Opportunities in 2026

Most people spend 18 months researching franchise opportunities and still pick the wrong one.

Not because there are too many options — there are over 3,000 franchise brands in the U.S. — but because they are researching the wrong things in the wrong order.

Here is the exact process we use with our clients to cut that 18 months down to 60 days and end up with a shortlist of 3 brands that actually fit their life.

## Step 1: Define Your Investment Profile Before You Look at Brands

Before you research a single franchise, get clarity on four numbers:

- **Liquid capital available**: What you can invest without touching retirement accounts
- **Total investment budget**: Including loans, SBA, HELOC — your maximum
- **Monthly income requirement**: What you need to live during the ramp-up period (typically 12-18 months)
- **Break-even timeline**: How long you can operate before the business must be cash-flow positive

Most buyers skip this step. They fall in love with a brand, then discover they can't afford it — or worse, they stretch into a franchise that stresses them financially from day one.

Your investment profile determines your category. $150K liquid gets you into service businesses. $300K opens up fitness, food, and multi-unit opportunities. Know your number first.

## Step 2: Match Business Model to Your Skills and Lifestyle

Franchises fail when there is a mismatch between the owner and the model. Ask yourself:

**Operational style:**
- Do you want to manage a team or work solo?
- Are you comfortable with physical space (retail, brick-and-mortar) or do you prefer home-based and mobile models?
- Can you handle variable revenue, or do you need subscription/recurring income?

**Time commitment:**
- Owner-operated vs. semi-absentee — these are fundamentally different businesses
- Some franchises require 60+ hours per week in year one. Others can be run with 10 hours per week from day one.

**Skills match:**
- Sales-driven models (consulting, B2B services) require comfort with outbound prospecting
- Operations-heavy models (food, fitness) require staff management
- Technology-forward models require adaptability

The brands that match your profile are your universe. Research everything else is noise.

## Step 3: Use a Franchise Consultant (and Understand What They Do)

Franchise consultants are free to buyers. They are paid by franchisors when a placement is made.

This creates an obvious question: can you trust their recommendations?

Yes — if you choose the right consultant. Here is what separates good consultants from order-takers:

- They ask about your finances and lifestyle before mentioning any brands
- They present options across multiple investment levels and categories
- They tell you which brands they have seen struggle and why
- They do not pressure you toward any specific system

A good consultant cuts your research time significantly because they have vetted hundreds of brands and know which ones have strong franchisee satisfaction, tight operations support, and realistic Item 19 disclosures.

## Step 4: Request FDDs and Read These Items First

Once you have a shortlist of 3-5 brands, request the Franchise Disclosure Document from each.

Every FDD has 23 Items. Do not read them in order. Start here:

**Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations**
This is the only Item that shows you what franchisees actually earn. Not every franchisor includes it — and the ones that don't are telling you something. Look for:
- Average gross revenue by unit age (Year 1, Year 2, Year 3+)
- Median unit performance (not just averages — outliers distort the mean)
- Percentage of units at or above the median

**Item 20 — Outlets and Franchisee Information**
- How many locations opened vs. closed in the last 3 years?
- What is the closure rate? (Closures ÷ total units)
- Is the system growing or contracting?

**Item 21 — Financial Statements**
- Is the franchisor profitable at the corporate level?
- Are they cash-flow positive?
- What is their audit history?

**Item 3 — Litigation**
- How many lawsuits have been filed against the franchisor by franchisees?
- Are there patterns — multiple suits on the same issue?

## Step 5: Talk to Current and Former Franchisees

Item 20 includes a full list of current franchisees with contact information. Call them.

The questions that matter:

1. If you were starting over, would you choose this franchise again?
2. How long did it take to hit break-even?
3. What did corporate support look like in your first 6 months?
4. What do you wish you had known before signing?
5. Have there been any major changes to the system since you joined?

Call 10 franchisees minimum. Former franchisees are even more valuable — they have nothing to protect. Find their contact information in the Item 20 transfer list.

## Step 6: Visit the Brand's Discovery Day

Most franchisors invite finalists to a Discovery Day at corporate headquarters. This is your chance to:

- Meet the leadership team in person
- See operations, training facilities, and support infrastructure
- Ask the hard questions face-to-face
- Read the room — is this a culture you want to be part of?

Attend Discovery Day only after you have read the FDD and talked to franchisees. You will ask better questions and evaluate the experience more clearly.

## Step 7: Hire a Franchise Attorney Before You Sign

A franchise attorney — not a general business attorney — should review your franchise agreement before you sign.

Key areas to review:

- **Territory protection**: Is your territory exclusive? What are the encroachment provisions?
- **Transfer rights**: How can you sell your franchise in the future?
- **Renewal terms**: What are the conditions for renewal? Can they change the terms?
- **Exit clauses**: What happens if you need to exit the agreement?

Attorney fees for FDD review typically run $1,500-$3,000. This is the cheapest due diligence you will ever do on a $200,000+ investment.

## The Research Timeline

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1-2 | Define investment profile, engage consultant |
| 3-4 | Review 10-15 brands, narrow to 5 |
| 5-6 | Request FDDs, read Items 19, 20, 21, 3 |
| 7-8 | Call 10+ franchisees per brand, narrow to 2-3 |
| 9-10 | Attend Discovery Days |
| 11-12 | Franchise attorney review, final decision |

Sixty days. That is a realistic timeline for a thorough process. Anything faster and you are skipping steps. Anything longer and you are overthinking it.

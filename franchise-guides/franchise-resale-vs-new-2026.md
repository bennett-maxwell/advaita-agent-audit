# Franchise Resale vs. New Franchise: Which Is the Better Investment in 2026?

*By FranchiseKI | Updated May 2026*

When people think about buying a franchise, they typically imagine a new location. But franchise resales — purchasing an existing franchise unit from a current owner — represent a significant and often overlooked opportunity. Here's how to evaluate both options.

## What Is a Franchise Resale?

A franchise resale is when an existing franchisee sells their operating unit(s) to a new buyer. The buyer takes over an established business with existing customers, staff, revenue, and cash flow — not starting from zero.

Franchise resales account for approximately 30–40% of franchise transactions. Many are never publicly listed — they're sold through franchise brokers or directly through the franchisor's transfer process.

## New Franchise vs. Resale: Key Differences

| Factor | New Franchise | Resale |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Initial setup | Build from scratch | Take over existing operations |
| Revenue from day 1 | Usually 0 | Often immediate |
| Validation | Based on other units' data | Based on this unit's actual history |
| Negotiation | Set franchise fee | Negotiable (may include goodwill premium) |
| Territory | Choose your market | Inherit existing territory |
| Staff | Hire new | Inherit existing team |
| Customer base | Build from zero | Existing customers |
| Capex | Full build-out | May need updates/renovations |

## Advantages of Franchise Resales

### 1. Existing Cash Flow
The most significant advantage: a resale may generate revenue on Day 1. If you're buying a healthy business, you may start servicing debt immediately rather than burning through working capital during ramp-up.

### 2. Proven Unit Economics
With a resale, you can see actual P&L statements — not just system averages. Ask for 3 years of financial records. This is more valuable than any Item 19 projection.

### 3. Existing Staff and Culture
Inheriting a trained team reduces your hiring and training costs. If key staff stay through the transition, you maintain operational continuity.

### 4. Immediate Visibility
You're not building brand awareness from scratch. The location already has foot traffic (for physical concepts) or an existing customer list (for service concepts).

### 5. Franchisor Confidence
Franchisors often view resale buyers favorably — the brand is already established in that territory. Franchisor support through a resale transition is typically strong.

## Disadvantages of Franchise Resales

### 1. Hidden Issues
You may be inheriting problems: unhappy staff, unresolved equipment issues, negative reviews, or undisclosed operational challenges. Due diligence must be thorough.

### 2. Premium Pricing
Sellers often price resales based on goodwill (value of existing business beyond hard assets). EBITDA multiples for small businesses typically range from 2–4x.

### 3. Limited Territory Options
You're buying a specific unit in a specific territory. If you want a different market, resale options may be limited.

### 4. Seller Motivation Matters
Why is the seller selling? Retirement and relocation are positive reasons. Health issues are neutral. Declining business performance is a major red flag.

### 5. Franchisor Approval Required
Any franchise transfer requires franchisor approval. The new buyer must meet the franchisor's financial and operational standards.

## How to Evaluate a Franchise Resale

**Step 1: Request the financials.** 3 years of P&L, tax returns, and a current balance sheet. Have a CPA review them.

**Step 2: Analyze the trend.** Is revenue growing, flat, or declining? Understand why.

**Step 3: Validate the customer base.** For service businesses, understand customer concentration. Is revenue from 1 client who might leave?

**Step 4: Inspect the assets.** Equipment condition, lease terms remaining, leasehold improvement value.

**Step 5: Talk to the staff.** Are key employees planning to stay?

**Step 6: Review the lease.** How long is the lease? What are the renewal options? A short remaining lease is a major risk.

**Step 7: Get the FDD.** The current FDD reflects the brand's current state, not when the unit was originally opened.

## Pricing a Franchise Resale

Resale pricing typically uses one of these methods:
- **Revenue multiple:** 0.5–1.5x annual revenue
- **EBITDA multiple:** 2–4x EBITDA (most common for established businesses)
- **Asset value:** Value of equipment, inventory, and leasehold improvements

Distressed sellers may price below asset value. Strong performers with growing revenue command EBITDA premiums.

## The FranchiseKI Recommendation

For buyers with limited time to ramp up a new location or who need earlier cash flow, resales are worth serious consideration. For buyers who want to choose their market and build their culture from day one, a new franchise unit may be better.

# How Long Does It Take to Buy a Franchise? Complete 2026 Timeline

*By FranchiseKI | Updated May 2026 | franchiseki.com*

---

One of the most common questions prospective franchise buyers ask: "How long will this take?" The honest answer: 60 to 180 days from first inquiry to signing, depending on the franchise system, your decision speed, and financing.

Here's the complete step-by-step timeline so you know exactly what to expect.

## The Complete Franchise Buying Timeline

### Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1–4)

**Week 1–2: Initial Research**
- Identify industries and investment ranges that fit your capital and lifestyle goals
- Connect with a franchise consultant (free service, paid by franchisors)
- Begin shortlisting 5–10 concepts based on your criteria

**Week 3–4: Introduction Calls**
- Introductory calls with 2–4 franchisors
- Receive and sign FDD Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) for systems of interest
- Receive the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD) from interested franchisors

**Timeline note:** Legally, franchisors must give you the FDD at least 14 calendar days before you can sign any agreement or pay any money (FTC Franchise Rule). This 14-day "cooling off" period is mandatory.

---

### Phase 2: Due Diligence (Weeks 4–10)

**FDD Review (2–3 weeks)**
- Read all 23 Items of the FDD carefully
- Key items: Item 7 (investment range), Item 12 (territory), Item 19 (financial performance), Item 20 (franchisee contacts), Item 21 (audited financials)
- Hire a franchise attorney ($1,500–$5,000 typical cost) to review the agreement

**Franchisee Validation Calls (2–3 weeks, overlapping)**
- Call 10–20 existing franchisees from Item 20
- Ask: revenue, satisfaction, what they'd do differently, would they buy again
- Visit 2–3 open locations if possible

**Discovery Day (typically Week 6–8)**
- Most franchise systems invite serious candidates to their headquarters for a 1–2 day Discovery Day
- Meet the leadership team, operations team, support staff
- Tour a model location
- This is also where they evaluate you as a franchisee candidate

---

### Phase 3: Financing (Weeks 6–12, overlapping)

**SBA Loan Process: 30–90 Days**
- Most franchise investments are financed via SBA 7(a) loans
- Typical approval timeline: 45–75 days from application to close
- Start financing parallel to due diligence—don't wait

**Documents typically needed:**
- 2–3 years personal tax returns
- Personal financial statement
- Business plan (some lenders require, others don't for SBA-approved franchises)
- Letter of intent from franchisor

**Alternative financing:**
- Rollover for Business Startups (ROBS) — use 401K/IRA without early withdrawal penalty
- Home equity line of credit (HELOC)
- Family/partner capital

---

### Phase 4: Territory Selection and Approval (Weeks 8–14)

**Territory Research**
- Review territory maps, demographic data, and competitive analysis
- Confirm desired territory is available
- Some franchisors provide territory analysis; others require you to research

**Franchisee Application and Approval**
- Submit formal franchisee application
- Background check, credit review, financial qualification
- Some systems have formal approval boards; others are more straightforward
- Approval typically takes 1–3 weeks once all documents are submitted

---

### Phase 5: Signing and Training (Weeks 12–18)

**Franchise Agreement Signing**
- Once approved and financing confirmed, you sign the Franchise Agreement
- At signing: pay franchise fee (typically $20K–$80K)
- Begin initial training (usually required before opening)

**Initial Training**
- Most systems: 2–6 weeks of training at headquarters + field training
- Some systems offer virtual + in-person hybrid

**Site Selection (retail/food concepts)**
- For brick-and-mortar locations: site selection is ongoing during due diligence
- Average time from lease signing to opening: 90–180 days (buildout + permits)

## Realistic Timeline Summary

| Scenario | Timeline |
|----------|----------|
| Fast decision, no financing needed | 60–90 days to signing |
| Typical buyer with SBA financing | 90–120 days to signing |
| Complex deal or slow decision process | 120–180 days to signing |
| From signing to opening (retail/food) | Add 90–180 days for buildout |

**Average across FranchiseKI placements: ~120 days from first conversation to signed agreement.**

## What Slows the Process Down

**1. Waiting on FDD review.** The 14-day mandatory waiting period can't be shortened—start your attorney review immediately.

**2. Delayed financing.** SBA loans take 45–75 days. Starting financing late delays everything.

**3. Indecision on territory.** Desired territories can sell while you're deciding. Have a backup.

**4. Not doing franchisee calls early.** Validation calls often surface deal-killers. Do them early, not late.

**5. Going it alone.** Working without a franchise consultant adds weeks to the process. Consultants front-load the research and shortlisting.

# Top 10 Franchise Industries in 2026: Growth, ROI, and What the Data Says

*Published by Franchise KI | May 2026 | 11 min read*

## Which Industries Are Winning in 2026?

Not all franchise industries grow at the same rate. Consumer preferences, demographics, AI adoption, and macroeconomic conditions create clear winners and losers in any given period.

This guide analyzes the top 10 franchise industries in 2026 by three metrics: revenue growth, franchisee satisfaction (from Item 20 analysis), and resilience to AI disruption.

---

## Methodology

Data sourced from:
- IFA (International Franchise Association) 2025-2026 Economic Outlook
- FDD Item 19 analysis across 3,239 brands in the Franchise KI database
- Bureau of Labor Statistics industry employment data
- Verified franchisee surveys (n=2,400, 2025)

---

## #1: Home Services (Growth: 12% YoY)

**Why it's #1 in 2026:**

The home services sector is experiencing a structural boom driven by two converging forces: an aging housing stock (average US home is 39 years old) and a shortage of skilled tradespeople. Homeowners can't find plumbers, painters, and cleaners—and they're willing to pay premium prices for trusted brands.

**Key data:**
- US home services market: $600B annually (IBIS World, 2025)
- Franchise penetration: only 11% (massive runway)
- Average AUV: $850K-$1.4M (commercial cleaning, restoration)
- Recession performance: maintained 8% growth during 2020 recession

**Best franchise categories:** Restoration/remediation, commercial cleaning, pest control, home inspection.

**AI resilience:** HIGH. Home services require physical labor that cannot be automated. AI is enhancing scheduling, customer communication, and routing—but the core work remains human.

---

## #2: Health, Wellness, and Senior Care (Growth: 11% YoY)

**Why it's growing:** The 73 million Baby Boomers are entering their peak healthcare consumption years. Senior care is the fastest-growing franchise subcategory—demand is literally demographic.

**Key data:**
- US senior population reaches 72M by 2030
- In-home senior care market: $130B by 2027 (Grand View Research)
- Franchise satisfaction: highest in IFA surveys for 3rd consecutive year

**Best franchise categories:** In-home senior care, memory care, medical staffing, fitness concepts.

**AI resilience:** MIXED. Some administrative functions automated; core care remains human.

---

## #3: Food & Beverage - Fast Casual (Growth: 9% YoY)

**Why it's winning:** Fast casual continues to steal share from QSR (quick service) and full-service restaurants. Consumers want customization, quality, and speed—fast casual delivers all three at accessible price points.

**Key data:**
- Fast casual market size: $209B globally (2025)
- Average AUV: $750K-$1.3M
- Digital ordering now 35-45% of revenue (up from 15% in 2020)

**Best franchise categories:** Salad and grain bowls, global street food, build-your-own concepts.

**Salad House example:** $35,000 franchise fee, 6% royalty, 40+ locations. AUV detailed in FDD Item 19. *States blocked: CT, IL, MN, WA. See FDD for details.*

---

## #4: Curated Retail / Community Commerce (Growth: 8% YoY)

**Why it's emerging:** The "doom loop" of traditional retail (department stores, big-box) has created massive opportunity for community-oriented retail concepts. Curated thrift, artisan markets, and specialty retail with community angles are filling the void.

**Key data:**
- US resale market: $64B (growing 15% YoY, ThredUp 2025)
- Booth rental model: eliminates inventory risk entirely
- Average time commitment: 10-20 hours/week (semi-absentee)

**Best franchise categories:** Curated consignment/thrift (Indy Clover model), specialty retail, artisan food markets.

**Indy Clover example:** Total investment $165K-$225K, Item 19 median net $104K/year, SBA-approved February 2026. *States blocked: TX, NC, SC, FL, KY, ME, OR, UT. See FDD.*

**AI resilience:** HIGH. Community and curation are fundamentally human. AI enhances inventory management and marketing.

---

## #5: Education and STEM (Growth: 8% YoY)

**Why it's growing:** Parental anxiety about academic preparation, AI literacy, and college admissions drives demand for supplemental education. STEM-focused concepts are particularly strong as coding, robotics, and AI skills become mandatory.

**Key data:**
- Supplemental education market: $125B US (2025)
- STEM franchise growth: outpacing general education by 3:1
- Customer retention: 85%+ annual renewal rates (high LTV)

---

## #6: Pet Services (Growth: 7% YoY)

**Why it's winning:** Pet ownership reached an all-time high post-pandemic. The humanization of pets—owners spending on grooming, training, daycare, and premium food—drives extraordinary LTV.

**Key data:**
- US pet industry: $148B (2025)
- Average pet owner lifetime spend: $35,000-$55,000
- Recession performance: pet spending held flat in 2008-2009 and 2020

---

## #7: Business Services / B2B (Growth: 7% YoY)

**Why it's growing:** The small business formation boom (5.5M new businesses in 2023, record high) creates demand for B2B services: bookkeeping, payroll, HR, marketing, staffing.

**Advantage for franchisees:** B2B contracts are recurring, larger than consumer transactions, and have lower churn. A single commercial cleaning contract might be $2,000-$5,000/month.

---

## #8: Children's Services (Growth: 6% YoY)

**Why it's resilient:** Parents spend more per child as family sizes shrink. Children's enrichment, sports, tutoring, and developmental programs benefit from intense parental investment in each child.

---

## #9: Technology Services (Growth: 6% YoY)

**Why it's emerging as a franchise category:** AI and tech adoption by small businesses is creating demand for local tech support, digital marketing services, and cybersecurity. Franchise concepts packaging these services are emerging.

---

## #10: Fitness and Wellness (Growth: 5% YoY)

**Note on recovery:** The fitness industry is still completing its post-COVID recovery. Studios that embraced hybrid models (in-person + digital) performed significantly better.

**Best franchise categories:** Boutique fitness (HIIT, cycling, yoga), mental wellness, recovery concepts (cryotherapy, infrared sauna).

---

## Industries to Watch (Early-Stage, High Potential)

**AI Business Services:** Franchise concepts helping SMBs adopt AI tools. Enormous potential, few established players.

**EV Charging Networks:** Still early but demographic trends favor franchise-able charging station networks.

**Mental Health Services:** Significant unmet demand, increasing insurance parity, limited competition.

---

## How to Use This Data in Your Franchise Search

**Match industry trends to your personal goals:**
- Semi-absentee lifestyle → Home services (manager-operated), curated retail, B2B
- Community-driven → Curated retail, children's services, fitness
- Highest AUV → Restoration/remediation, commercial cleaning, fast casual

**Check the specific brand, not just the category:**
Two brands in the same industry can have wildly different franchisee outcomes. The industry growth trend helps you select a category—but the FDD, Item 19 data, and franchisee validation determine your specific investment decision.

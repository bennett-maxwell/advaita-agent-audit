## What Is a Franchise Broker?

A franchise broker (also called a franchise consultant) connects prospective franchise buyers with franchise opportunities. Unlike a real estate agent, a franchise broker is paid exclusively by the franchisor — meaning the service is **free for buyers.**

This creates an important dynamic: some brokers prioritize the highest commission brands, not the best fit for the buyer. The top franchise brokers overcome this through technology, transparency, and data-driven matching.

## Questions to Ask Every Franchise Broker

Before committing to work with any broker, ask:

**1. "How are you compensated, and by whom?"**
Right answer: "I'm paid a referral fee by the franchisor when a deal closes. Here's the typical range."
Red flag: Vague or evasive responses.

**2. "How many brands do you actively represent?"**
Right answer: Anything from 200-3,000+ with verified data
Red flag: "We work with the top brands" without specifics

**3. "Can you show me Item 19 financial data before I talk to any franchisor?"**
Right answer: "Yes — here's what we have in our system for franchises matching your criteria."
Red flag: "The franchisor will share that during the discovery call."

**4. "What's your process if a franchise I'm interested in isn't in your portfolio?"**
Right answer: "We can still help you evaluate it independently."
Red flag: "We only recommend brands we represent."

**5. "Can I speak with buyers you've placed in the last 12 months?"**
Right answer: "Yes — I'll send you references by tomorrow."
Red flag: Hesitation or excuses.

## Red Flags: Franchise Brokers to Avoid

**The Pusher:** Immediately recommends 1-2 specific brands without learning about you. (They earn the highest fee on those brands.)

**The Closer:** Creates urgency — "This territory won't be available next week!" Pressure is a manipulation tactic.

**The Information Hoarder:** Won't share FDD financials until you're in the franchisor's sales process. A good broker shows you the data first.

**The Nondisclosure Artist:** Won't tell you what commission they earn on different brands. This is basic transparency.

**The Generalist:** Claims expertise in every industry category. Deep knowledge matters — a broker who knows QSR, cleaning, and fitness equally well knows none of them well.

## How Franchise Broker Fees Work

**Franchisor pays:** Typically 40-50% of the initial franchise fee, paid when the deal closes. A $50,000 franchise fee = $25,000 broker commission.

**Buyer pays:** Nothing. The broker's fee is built into the franchisor's cost structure and doesn't increase what you pay for the franchise.

**Conflict of interest:** Brokers earn more on higher-fee franchises. The best brokers disclose their fees and match you on fit, not fee size.

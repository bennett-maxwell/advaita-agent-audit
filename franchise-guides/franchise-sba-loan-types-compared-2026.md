# SBA Loan Types for Franchise Financing: 7(a) vs 504 Compared (2026)

SBA loans are among the most popular financing tools for franchise buyers. Understanding the difference between the 7(a) and 504 programs — and when to use each — helps you structure capital more effectively from day one.

## SBA 7(a): The Versatile Workhorse

The SBA 7(a) is the most commonly used loan for franchise financing. It can fund working capital, equipment, leasehold improvements, and in some cases franchise fees. Terms typically extend up to 10 years for working capital and up to 25 years for real estate components*.

Key features:
- **Use of proceeds**: Broad — covers franchise fee, working capital, equipment, buildout
- **Down payment**: Typically 10%–20%* of total project cost
- **Personal guarantee**: Required from owners with 20%+ equity stake
- **SBA Franchise Registry**: Verify your target franchise is SBA-registered to streamline approval

*Loan terms, rates, and requirements vary by lender and are subject to SBA program guidelines. Consult a qualified SBA lender for current terms.*

## SBA 504: Best for Real Estate-Heavy Concepts

The 504 program is ideal when significant real property or long-life equipment is involved — think food concepts with owned buildings or industrial service franchises.

Key features:
- **Structure**: Borrower contributes 10%, Certified Development Company (CDC) funds 40%, private lender funds 50%
- **Best for**: Brick-and-mortar concepts purchasing their location rather than leasing
- **Limitation**: Cannot fund working capital or franchise fees directly

## Choosing Between Them

Most franchisees starting with a leased location use the 7(a). Franchisees acquiring real property with a large equipment or real estate component may benefit from the 504 structure. Some sophisticated buyers use both programs simultaneously for different tranches of their project.

## SBA Approval and Franchise Registry

Franchises listed on the SBA Franchise Registry receive streamlined lender review. If your target brand is not listed, expect additional underwriting documentation requirements. Confirm registry status before entering SBA pre-qualification.

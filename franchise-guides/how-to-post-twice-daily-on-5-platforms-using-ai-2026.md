# How I'm Posting Twice a Day on 5 Platforms Without Recording Anything New

*Published by Franchise KI | Updated May 2026*

People ask me how I have time to post on LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and X twice a day while running a franchise advisory firm, building a franchise directory with 3,000+ brands, and managing an AI agent fleet.

The answer: I don't have time. My AI handles it.

Here's the exact system.

## The 60-30-10 Framework

Every piece of content I post falls into one of three categories:

**60% — How-To Strategy**: "Here's how AI works for YOUR business." The practical stuff — how to build an agent, what mistakes to avoid, how to think about automation.

**30% — Examples and Demonstrations**: Real outputs. Screenshots of dashboards. "My AI just did X." Skill spotlights showing specific tools I've built. This is where people see the proof before they believe the strategy.

**10% — Success Stories**: Results. Numbers. What the AI has actually produced. But only 10% — because if all you post is success stories, people stop trusting you.

At 14 posts per week, this breaks down to: 8-9 strategy posts, 4 example posts, 1-2 success stories.

## The 4 Content Pillars

I've organized everything into four buckets that rotate through the week:

### Pillar A: Build Log (3x/week)
Real-time documentation of what I'm building. Right now that's MapKi (mapki.franchiseki.com) — a franchise directory with FDD-verified data on 3,239 brands. Every sprint becomes a Build Log post.

Build Log #1 was the origin story — how my former business partner said "I was working on what started as a map tool" and we decided to build the Zillow of buying a franchise.

Build Log posts are naturally serialized. They create anticipation. People want to see the next update.

### Pillar B: AI Thesis (3x/week)
The philosophy posts. Short. Punchy. Authentic.

These are the posts that say things like:
- "You're not automating tasks. You're training an employee that sticks with you forever and can be applied across every business you ever build."
- "Nothing else matters to me right now. Not because it's trendy. Because I genuinely believe the businesses that figure out AI agents in the next 18 months will have an insurmountable advantage."

All of this is real. It's what I actually think. The posts come from my voice recordings — I've been talking about this in my car, on walks, in meetings for six months.

The voice-mining-skill I've built turns those recordings into structured posts automatically.

### Pillar C: Skill Drop (2x/week)
The engagement mechanic that drives the most comments.

"Comment PUSH HARDER and I'll DM you the full skill playbook."

I've built 80 skills. Each skill becomes a Skill Drop post. That's 40 weeks of content at two drops per week — nearly a year without repeating.

When someone comments, LinkedIn shows that post to more people. The DM creates a 1-on-1 relationship. It's the highest-ROI content format on LinkedIn right now.

### Pillar D: Results (2x/week)
The proof posts. Screenshots of the finance dashboard. GHL pipeline data. "We published 59 franchise research articles in 30 days." Real outputs from real work.

People believe strategy after they see results. Results posts prime the audience for everything else.

## How It Actually Gets Produced

**Every Monday**, my AI (Squirrel) runs the voice-mining-skill:
1. Downloads 3-5 recent voice recordings from Drive
2. Extracts 7 content types per recording: explanations, data points, personal moments, teaching moments, decision rationales, opinions, frustrations
3. Drafts 15 posts covering all 4 pillars
4. Routes them to the Social Queue document
5. Sends the queue to my social media manager (Christelle) for scheduling

**Christelle** handles Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and X via Metricool.
**Squirrel** handles LinkedIn directly (text posts go live automatically with 20+ hour spacing between posts).

I review nothing. The content came from my recordings. The voice is mine. The distribution is automated.

## The MapKi Build Log as Ongoing Content

MapKi is the best content asset I've ever built — not because it generates leads (yet), but because every improvement creates a post.

- "Build Log #1: The map tool idea (March 23, 2026)"
- "Build Log #3: FranchiseScore — we're building the Zestimate for franchises"
- "Build Log #7: 59 articles in 30 days, zero AI search citations yet"

The audience grows alongside the product. By the time MapKi has significant traffic, I'll have an audience of people who watched it get built.

This is the meta-strategy: **build something real, document it publicly, let the documentation become the marketing.**

## The Skill Drop Mechanic in Detail

The DM mechanic works because LinkedIn rewards comments more than likes. When someone types "COUNCIL" on a post, that triggers a comment notification for everyone who follows me. The algorithm shows it to more people. Then I DM them the framework.

I have 80 skills. The top 15 I'm releasing publicly in the first 8 weeks:
1. push-harder-skill (PUSH HARDER)
2. council-skill (COUNCIL)
3. autopilot-skill (AUTOPILOT)
4. voice-mining-skill (VOICE MINING)
5. mapki-skill (MAPKI)
...and 10 more through Week 8.

After Week 8, I'll assess which skills drove the most engagement and double down on those verticals.

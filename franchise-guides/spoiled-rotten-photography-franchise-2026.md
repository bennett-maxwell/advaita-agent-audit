# Spoiled Rotten Photography Franchise Review 2026: School Portrait Business Model

## What Is Spoiled Rotten Photography?

Spoiled Rotten Photography (SRP) is a boutique school photography franchise that specializes in on-location portrait photography for preschools, daycares, and elementary schools. Unlike traditional school photography companies that use volume-based commodity shooting, SRP focuses on high-quality, styled portraits that parents actually want to buy.

Founded in 2009, SRP has built a national franchise network operating in 30+ states.

## The Business Model

SRP franchisees:
1. Contract with preschools and daycares as their exclusive photography provider
2. Go on-site to shoot portrait sessions (typically 1-3 days per school per year)
3. Parents order prints online through SRP's proprietary ordering system
4. Franchisee receives a revenue share from print sales

Revenue drivers: Recurring school contracts, parent print orders, seasonal mini sessions.

## Why Schools Love SRP

High school retention rates (70%+ annual renewal in established territories). On-location, natural-light sessions, lower pressure ordering model, relationship-based partnerships.

## Who Is the Ideal SRP Franchisee?

SRP targets owners who have a passion for photography and working with children, want a flexible schedule business (((sessions are pre-scheduled)), are community-connected, and can manage a small team. No physical location required - making it capital-efficient.

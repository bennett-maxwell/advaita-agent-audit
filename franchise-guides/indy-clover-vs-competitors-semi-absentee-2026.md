- **AUV:** $593,000/year (sharpsheets.io, 2025 FDD data)
- **Item 19 median net:** $104,000/year
- **Time commitment:** 10-20 hours/week (semi-absentee by design)
- **SBA approved:** February 2026
- **Locations:** 16+ across 7 states

## How Indy Clover Compares to Other Semi-Absentee Concepts

### Indy Clover vs. Flip Flop Shops

| Factor | Indy Clover | Flip Flop Shops |

| Model | Booth rental (no inventory) | Retail footwear (inventory-heavy) |

### Indy Clover vs. Bin There Dump That

| Factor | Indy Clover | Bin There Dump That |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Investment | $159K–$216K | $86K–$175K |
| Model | Community retail | Home services (dumpster rental) |
| Revenue type | Weekly booth rental | Per-job project revenue |
| Time commitment | 10-20 hrs/week | Variable (can be semi-absentee) |
| Market size | $50B+ resale market | Home renovation spending |

**Edge: Depends.** If you want lower entry and don't mind physical operations (trucks), Bin There is interesting. If you want a community hub with recurring booth rental income, Indy Clover wins.

### Indy Clover vs. Plato's Closet / Once Upon a Child (Winmark)

| Factor | Indy Clover | Winmark Concepts |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Investment | $159K–$216K | $250K–$500K |
| Model | Booth rental (no inventory buying) | Store buys and resells used goods |
| Inventory risk | None (sellers own it) | High (store buys everything) |
| Staff requirements | Minimal | Higher (buying staff needed) |
| Recession resistance | Strong | Strong |
| Brand recognition | Growing | Established (40+ years) |

**Edge: Indy Clover for lower risk.** Winmark brands are well-established but require the store to buy inventory — taking on pricing and selection risk. Indy Clover's booth model eliminates this entirely.

## Who Is the Ideal Indy Clover Franchisee?

FranchiseKI has worked with many Indy Clover buyers. The profile that thrives:

- **W2 professional** who wants a side business that runs mostly without them
- **Community-oriented** — loves the idea of supporting local sellers
- **Capital-ready** at $70K–$150K liquid with access to SBA financing
- **Market-aware** — they see their city's resale and thrift culture growing
- **NOT looking for:** the fastest growth or highest potential ceiling. Indy Clover is about stable, predictable returns with low owner involvement.

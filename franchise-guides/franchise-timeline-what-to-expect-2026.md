# Franchise Timeline: What to Expect from Discovery to Opening Day (2026)

One of the most common questions from franchise candidates: "How long does this actually take?"

The honest answer: 90 to 180 days from first call to open doors, depending on the brand and your readiness. Here's the exact roadmap.

## The 6 Phases of Buying a Franchise

### Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1-3)

- Initial franchise fit call with broker or franchisor
- Review Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD) when received
- Territory availability check
- Demographic report review

**What moves this forward:** Being responsive, having clear territory preferences, confirming liquid capital.

### Phase 2: Financial Qualification (Weeks 3-5)

- Confirm liquid capital (cash, retirement accounts, equity)
- Pre-qualify for SBA loan if needed (30-60 days for full approval)
- Review Item 19 of the FDD (financial performance representations)
- Independent attorney FDD review recommended

**Minimum liquid for top brands:**
- Indy Clover: $70,000
- Salad House: $125,000-$150,000
- Spoiled Rotten Photography: $50,000

### Phase 3: Validation (Weeks 4-6)

- Speak with current franchisees (Item 20 of FDD lists contacts)
- Ask the real questions: What would you do differently? What surprised you?
- Review the brand's Item 19 earnings data with an accountant

### Phase 4: Discovery Day (Week 6-8)

- Visit headquarters or brand hub
- Meet the executive team
- See operations firsthand
- Make your go/no-go decision

### Phase 5: Signing + Financing (Weeks 8-14)

- Sign Franchise Agreement
- Pay franchise fee ($25,000-$50,000 typical)
- SBA loan closes (adds 30-60 days if not pre-qualified)
- Location selection begins (for brick-and-mortar)

### Phase 6: Buildout + Training (Weeks 12-24)

- Real estate selection and lease signing
- Construction / buildout (varies widely: 30-120 days)
- Training at corporate (1-3 weeks)
- Staff hiring and onboarding
- Soft open → grand open

## What Slows Everything Down

1. **Slow FDD review** — Don't sit on it. Read it immediately.
2. **Financing delays** — Start pre-qualification BEFORE you're ready to sign.
3. **Indecision on territory** — Have 3-5 options ready from day one.
4. **No attorney reviewed** — Some brands require it; all recommend it.
5. **Lease negotiation** — Can add 30-60 days in competitive markets.

## Semi-Absentee Brands Move Faster

Brands like Indy Clover (community hub / curated thrift) don't require real estate build-outs in the traditional sense. Timeline can compress to 60-90 days from discovery call to open doors.

## The Real Timeline by Brand Type

| Brand Type | Discovery to Open |
|------------|-------------------|
| Home services | 60-90 days |
| Semi-absentee retail (IC model) | 90-120 days |
| Food service / restaurant | 120-180 days |
| Fitness / health | 90-150 days |

## One Thing Most Candidates Get Wrong

They wait until they're "100% sure" to start the process. The reality: you get 100% sure BY going through the process. The FDD review, franchisee calls, and Discovery Day are designed to give you that certainty.

## Understanding How Franchise Buying Works

When you "buy a franchise," you're purchasing a license to operate a proven business model. The process involves:

1. **Paying an Initial Franchise Fee** — typically $20,000–$100,000
2. **Building out your location** — equipment, leasehold improvements, signage
3. **Working capital** — funding to operate before you break even
4. **Ongoing royalties** — typically 4-8% of gross revenue monthly
5. **Marketing fund contributions** — typically 1-3% of gross revenue

**Total initial investment** for most franchises ranges from $50,000 (home-based service) to $1,000,000+ (restaurant with real estate).

## The Franchise Buying Process: Step by Step

### Step 1: Self-Assessment (Week 1-2)

Before researching brands, honestly answer:
- How much liquid capital do you have? (Rule of thumb: you need 10-20% of total investment liquid)
- Do you want to be hands-on (owner-operator) or hands-off (semi-absentee)?
- What industries match your experience and interest?
- What's your income replacement target in years 1, 2, 3?
- Are you comfortable with employees? How many?
- What geographic territory do you want?

### Step 2: Initial Research (Week 2-4)

Use MapKi (mapki.franchiseki.com) to browse brands matching your capital and lifestyle criteria. Review:
- Total initial investment (not just franchise fee)
- Royalty structure
- Item 19 financial data (average unit volumes)
- Territory availability in your area
- Franchisor support model

### Step 3: Franchise Consultant Discovery Call (Week 3-5)

Book a free consultation with Franchise KI or another reputable franchise broker. Expect:
- 45-60 minute structured conversation
- Detailed profile assessment
- 3-7 franchise recommendations based on your profile

### Step 4: Brand Research and FDD Review (Week 4-10)

For each brand you're seriously considering:
- **Request the FDD** from the franchisor (they must provide it before you sign anything)
- Review **Item 19** carefully: what are the average, median, and range of unit revenues?
- **Speak with current franchisees** (listed in Item 20 of the FDD): ask about support, profitability, and what they'd do differently
- **Hire a franchise attorney** to review the FDD (cost: $1,500–$3,000; non-negotiable)

### Step 5: Territory and Location Analysis (Week 8-12)

- Confirm your desired territory is available
- For brick-and-mortar franchises: site selection, demographics analysis, traffic counts
- Lease negotiation support (many franchisors have real estate teams)

### Step 6: Financing (Week 6-14, runs parallel)

Options:
- **Cash investment** — liquid savings, 401(k) rollover (ROBS), home equity
- **SBA 7(a) loan** — most common; covers up to 90% of investment; franchise-approved lenders recommended
- **Franchisor financing** — some franchisors offer in-house financing or deferred fee programs

Get pre-qualified before signing anything.

### Step 7: Final Decision and Signing (Week 12-16+)

- Attend the franchise discovery day (required by most franchisors)
- Make your final decision
- Sign the franchise agreement (after attorney review)
- Pay initial franchise fee and begin build-out

## Red Flags: Franchises to Avoid

Watch out for:
- **Franchisors who won't provide Item 19 data** — if they claim "we don't disclose earnings," ask why
- **Very high churn in franchisee turnover** (check Item 20 of FDD)
- **Active litigation from franchisees** (check Item 3 of FDD)
- **New franchisors with under 10 operating units** — unproven model
- **High-pressure sales tactics** — "this territory won't last!" is a manipulation tactic

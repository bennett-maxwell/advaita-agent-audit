# 23 Questions to Ask Your Franchise Broker Before Signing Anything (2026)

Not all franchise brokers are created equal. The right broker finds you the right fit. The wrong broker finds you the highest commission. Here's how to tell the difference — and what to ask before you trust someone with your financial future.

## First: Understand How Brokers Get Paid

Most franchise brokers are paid by the franchisor — not by you. When a deal closes, the franchisor pays the broker a referral fee (typically $15,000-$40,000). This creates a real incentive to close, not to find your best fit.

Good brokers still serve your interests. Bad brokers just push toward brands that pay the highest commissions. Ask about this directly.

## 23 Questions That Separate Great Brokers from Average Ones

### About Their Experience and Inventory

1. How many brands do you represent? (More isn't always better — quality over quantity)
2. How do you choose which brands to include in your portfolio?
3. How many deals have you closed in the last 12 months?
4. What's the average investment range of the deals you close?
5. Do you specialize in any industry or investment level?

### About Their Process

6. How do you qualify a brand before recommending it?
7. Do you personally know the franchise development teams at the brands you recommend?
8. What does your discovery process look like — how many calls, how long, what's the deliverable?
9. Do you review the FDD with clients, or do you just hand it over?
10. Will you connect me with current franchisees before I move forward?

### About Conflicts of Interest

11. How are you compensated — by franchisors, by me, or both?
12. Are there brands you push more than others because of commission rates?
13. What happens if none of your brands are a good fit for me — do you refer me elsewhere?

### About the Brands They're Recommending

14. Why specifically is this brand a good fit for my situation?
15. What's the Item 19 data — the actual financial performance of existing franchisees?
16. What's the brand's franchisee satisfaction score? (Some franchisors share this data)
17. What's the franchisee turnover rate — how many have closed vs. total open locations?
18. Are there any lawsuits, regulatory actions, or FDD amendments I should know about?
19. What territories are actually available — and can you verify this?

### About Your Situation

20. Have you worked with people from my professional background before? What happened?
21. What franchise fits people who want to be semi-absentee vs. full-time operators?
22. What's realistic for a first-time franchise owner vs. someone with prior business experience?
23. What would disqualify me as a candidate, and are there any red flags you see?

## Green Flags in a Good Broker

- Tells you "this brand isn't right for you" before you invest
- Proactively shares negative information about brands
- Gives you franchisee contacts without being asked
- Stays in touch after the deal closes
- Has been in the business 5+ years with verifiable track record

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Pushes urgency ("This territory won't be available next week")
- Only recommends 1-2 brands for every buyer
- Won't share specific Item 19 earnings data
- Gets defensive when you ask about commission structures
- Discourages you from hiring your own attorney

## The Smart Way to Use a Broker

Treat your broker like a doctor. Tell them everything — your real financial situation, your lifestyle goals, your fears. The more they know, the better the fit they can find.

And always remember: the broker gets paid when you sign. Your job is to find the brand that serves YOUR goals, not theirs.

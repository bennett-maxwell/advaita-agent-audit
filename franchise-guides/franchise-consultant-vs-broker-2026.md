# Franchise Consultant vs Franchise Broker: What's the Difference in 2026?

*These terms are often used interchangeably — but there are important distinctions buyers should know.*

---

## Quick Answer

**Franchise broker** = typically refers to someone who earns commissions placing buyers with specific franchisors they represent.

**Franchise consultant** = typically refers to a more comprehensive advisory role, including analysis, education, FDD review guidance, and sometimes independent advice not tied to commission.

In practice, most professionals in this field do both — and the titles are largely interchangeable. **The more important distinction is: are they buyer-first, or commission-first?**

---

## The Commission Structure Problem

Both franchise brokers and consultants are typically paid by the franchisor when a deal closes:

- Franchise fee: $50,000
- Broker/consultant commission: 40-50% of franchise fee = $25,000

This creates a natural conflict: brokers earn more from recommending franchises with higher fees, regardless of whether that brand is the best fit for the buyer.

The best consultants and brokers overcome this by:
1. Transparent disclosure of their commission structure
2. Data-driven matching that doesn't prioritize fee size
3. Showing buyers verified financial data before the franchisor sales process begins

## The Right Questions to Ask

Whether you're talking to a broker or consultant, these questions cut through the noise:

1. **"Show me the Item 19 data for franchises matching my profile."** A legitimate consultant can do this. A commission-focused broker will defer to the franchisor.

2. **"What commission do you earn on this brand vs. that brand?"** The best consultants answer this directly.

3. **"Can I research independently on your platform before talking to any franchisor?"** MapKi is built for this. Most brokers send you directly into the sales funnel.

4. **"What would you tell a friend in my position who asked if this franchise was right for them?"** The answer reveals whether they're truly buyer-first.

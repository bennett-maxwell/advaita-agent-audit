# Franchise Consultant vs. Franchise Broker: What's the Difference in 2026?

The terms "franchise consultant" and "franchise broker" are often used interchangeably, but understanding the distinction helps buyers navigate the franchise acquisition process more effectively.

## What Is a Franchise Consultant?

A franchise consultant (also called a franchise advisor) guides prospective buyers through the entire process of evaluating, selecting, and purchasing a franchise. Their role is advisory and educational:

- Assess the buyer's financial profile, goals, and lifestyle preferences
- Match buyers with franchise concepts that align with their profile
- Explain FDD requirements and what to look for
- Coordinate introductions to franchise brands
- Support the buyer through validation and discovery day
- Help buyers understand financing options

**Key characteristic:** The consultant's primary obligation is to the buyer.

## What Is a Franchise Broker?

A franchise broker facilitates the transaction between a franchisor and a franchisee. While often used synonymously with "consultant," the broker role emphasizes the transactional aspect:

- Represents multiple franchise brands (typically 200-500+)
- Earns a commission from franchisors upon deal completion
- Introduces qualified buyers to appropriate brand opportunities

**Key characteristic:** The broker earns money from the transaction, though reputable brokers maintain buyer-first ethics.

## Who Pays the Franchise Consultant?

This is the most important question buyers ask—and the most misunderstood answer.

**Franchise consultants are paid by franchisors, not buyers.** The commission is built into the franchise fee that franchisors charge all new franchisees. Whether you use a consultant or approach a franchisor directly, the franchisee pays the same amount.

This means: **Using a qualified franchise consultant costs you nothing extra.**

Why would franchisors pay brokers? Because brokers pre-qualify buyers, saving franchisors significant time and money in their development process. A fringe sale to an unqualified buyer costs a franchisor far more in support costs and potential failure than a commission paid to a broker who delivered a well-matched franchisee.

## Questions to Ask Any Franchise Consultant

Before working with any franchise advisor, ask:

1. **How many brands do you represent?** (More isn't always better; depth matters more than breadth)
2. **Have you personally been a franchisee?** (Operational experience is invaluable)
3. **How do you get compensated?** (All legitimate consultants are franchisor-paid; anyone charging buyers upfront fees is a red flag)
4. **What's your process for franchise validation?** (The best consultants coach you on how to do thorough franchisee interviews)
5. **What happens if I want a brand you don't represent?** (Good consultants help you find the right fit, even if it means referring you elsewhere)

## Red Flags in the Franchise Consulting Industry

- Pushing only one or two brands regardless of buyer profile
- Rushing the timeline to close the deal before the buyer is ready
- Minimizing or avoiding discussion of FDD Item 19 (earnings)
- Discouraging independent legal review
- Downplaying the importance of franchisee validation calls

# The Franchise Consultant's Role: What We Actually Do (And What We Don't)

*By FranchiseKI | Updated May 2026 | franchiseki.com*

---

One of the most common misconceptions in franchise buying: "A franchise consultant is just a salesperson who pushes specific brands."

This guide explains what a legitimate franchise consultant actually does, how we get paid, what our incentives are, and when working with one genuinely helps you — versus when it doesn't.

## What Is a Franchise Consultant?

A franchise consultant (also called a franchise broker or franchise coach) is an independent advisor who helps prospective buyers:

1. **Clarify their goals** — capital, timeline, lifestyle, risk tolerance
2. **Identify matching concepts** — from a database of 100-500+ vetted franchise systems
3. **Navigate the due diligence process** — FDD review, franchisee calls, Discovery Days
4. **Connect with financing resources** — SBA lenders, ROBS providers, franchise-focused banks
5. **Avoid common mistakes** — undercapitalization, wrong territory, wrong match

Importantly: **a franchise consultant does not work for the franchisor.** We represent the buyer throughout the process.

## How Franchise Consultants Get Paid

This is the #1 question and the #1 source of confusion.

**Franchise consultants are paid by franchisors — not by you.**

When you purchase a franchise through a consultant, the franchisor pays a referral fee (typically 40-50% of the franchise fee). Your franchise fee is the same whether you buy directly or through a consultant. The fee is built into the system.

**Example:**
- Franchise fee: $40,000
- If you buy direct: you pay $40,000
- If you buy through FranchiseKI: you pay $40,000 (we receive ~$16,000–$20,000 from the franchisor)

**Your cost = $0.** The franchisor's cost = reduced marketing expenses.

## What This Means for Our Incentives

We're paid only when you complete a purchase — and only on brands in our portfolio. This creates two incentive concerns buyers should understand:

**1. We only show you brands we represent.** Most consultants work within a network (FranNet, FranChoice, FranchiseKI) with a portfolio of 100-400+ brands. We can't send you to a brand we don't have a relationship with. Ask your consultant: "How many brands are in your portfolio, and can you tell me about any you're NOT recommending and why?"

**2. Higher-fee brands pay us more.** A $60K franchise fee generates more for us than a $20K one. Ethical consultants still recommend lower-fee brands when they're the right fit — but be aware of this dynamic.

At FranchiseKI, our portfolio spans 150+ brands across investment levels and categories. We recommend what fits — not what pays most.

## What a Good Franchise Consultant Does For You

**1. Saves you 40-80 hours of research.** We've already reviewed hundreds of FDDs and know which systems have strong franchisee validation, which have litigation risks, and which are growing vs. declining.

**2. Coaches you on franchisee calls.** Knowing what to ask (and what to listen for) on those 10 validation calls makes a major difference. We coach you before and debrief after.

**3. Keeps you from falling in love.** Buyers sometimes fall in love with a concept's brand before doing the work. We're the objective voice reminding you: "The unit economics on this one are below average. Here's why."

**4. Coordinates Discovery Day logistics.** We facilitate introductions, prep you on what to expect, and help you evaluate what you heard.

**5. Knows the financing landscape.** We have relationships with SBA lenders and ROBS providers who specialize in franchise transactions. We know who is fast, who is difficult, and who to avoid.

## When You Don't Need a Franchise Consultant

There are legitimate cases where working with a consultant adds less value:

- **You already know exactly which franchise you want** and have already done extensive research. In this case, go direct and keep the franchise fee structure transparent.

- **You're buying a franchise resale** where you're working with a business broker on a specific listing. Franchise brokers focus on new unit sales; a business broker or M&A specialist is the right advisor for resales.

- **The specific franchise isn't in any consultant's portfolio.** Newer or smaller franchise systems may not have consultant relationships established yet.

## How to Evaluate a Franchise Consultant

**Ask these questions:**
1. How many brands are in your portfolio?
2. What's your process for recommending concepts vs. ones you don't recommend?
3. Do you have experience with buyers in my investment range and market?
4. Can you walk me through a deal you helped with where the buyer ultimately didn't buy — and why?

A great consultant should enthusiastically explain why some buyers should NOT buy a franchise, or should NOT buy a specific brand, even if it means no commission.

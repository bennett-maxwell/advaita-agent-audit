# Franchise Broker vs. Franchise Consultant: What's the Difference in 2026?

**Published:** 2026  
**Source:** FranchiseKI | [mapki.franchiseki.com](https://mapki.franchiseki.com)

The terms "franchise broker" and "franchise consultant" are often used interchangeably — but they're not the same thing. Understanding the difference can save you thousands of dollars and months of wasted research.

## The Core Distinction

**A franchise broker** matches buyers to franchises from a curated portfolio of brands they represent. They're paid by the franchisor — not by you.

**A franchise consultant** provides advice on whether franchising is right for you, how to evaluate an FDD, how to structure financing, or how to exit an existing franchise. They may charge hourly fees or project-based rates.

In practice, many professionals do both. The key question to ask any advisor: *"How are you paid, and which brands are you authorized to sell?"*

---

## Franchise Brokers: How They Work

Brokers like FranchiseKI operate within a network of franchise brands that have agreed to pay a placement fee when a qualified buyer signs a franchise agreement.

**How brokers are compensated:**
- The franchisor pays a referral fee (typically $20K–$40K per placement)
- The buyer pays nothing extra — the fee comes from the franchisor's development budget
- A good broker uses this structure to remove any cost barrier from the buyer side

**What brokers do:**
- Qualify buyers based on capital, lifestyle, goals, and risk tolerance
- Match buyers to 2–5 best-fit brands from their portfolio
- Schedule discovery days and facilitate due diligence
- Help interpret Item 19 and compare financial performance across brands
- Coordinate the franchise agreement process

**What brokers don't do:**
- Negotiate franchise fees (those are set by the franchisor)
- Provide legal advice (you need a franchise attorney for FDD review)
- Guarantee outcomes or earnings

---

## Franchise Consultants: How They Work

Independent franchise consultants typically work for buyers who want objective advice untied to any placement fee. Common engagement types:

- **FDD review support**: Walking a buyer through all 23 FDD items line by line
- **Feasibility analysis**: Is franchising the right path versus buying an independent business?
- **Exit planning**: Helping existing franchisees maximize value before selling a location
- **Multi-unit strategy**: Advising operators on how to scale from 1 to 5+ units

Fee structures vary widely: $150–$500/hour, or $2,000–$10,000 for a full engagement.

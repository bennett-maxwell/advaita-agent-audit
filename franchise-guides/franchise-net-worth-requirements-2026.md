# Franchise Net Worth Requirements Explained (2026)

Before awarding a franchise, most franchisors require candidates to meet minimum net worth thresholds. Understanding what counts toward net worth — and how franchisors verify it — is essential preparation for any serious buyer.

## What Is Net Worth in a Franchise Context?

Net worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. Franchisors care about net worth because it signals your ability to sustain the business through the early growth phase, fund unexpected expenses, and support multiple locations if you scale.

## Typical Net Worth Ranges by Investment Tier

Net worth requirements vary significantly by brand and concept type. As a general benchmark:
- Entry-level service franchises (liquid capital ~$50K–$100K per FDD Item 7*): net worth requirement often $100K–$300K*
- Mid-market franchises ($150K–$400K liquid*): net worth often $350K–$800K*
- Large format or multi-unit concepts ($500K+ liquid*): net worth often $1M+*

*All figures are illustrative ranges. Actual requirements are set by each franchisor and disclosed in Item 5/7 of their Franchise Disclosure Document. Requirements vary by brand, territory, and market conditions.*

## What Counts Toward Net Worth?

Assets typically included: primary residence equity, investment accounts, retirement accounts (at market value), business equity, real estate holdings, vehicles, and other tangible assets.

Assets typically excluded or discounted: personal property (jewelry, art), vested stock options, and assets with high liquidation costs.

## Liquid Capital vs. Net Worth: Key Distinction

Franchisors require BOTH. Liquid capital (cash, money market, stocks) must be immediately accessible. Net worth is the broader financial cushion. You can have high net worth but insufficient liquid capital — and vice versa. Both thresholds must be met independently.

## Verification Process

Franchisors typically request a personal financial statement during the discovery process. Some engage third-party financial verification services. Franchisors are NOT permitted to require bank statement disclosure during initial qualification stages (FDD Item 5 governs fees paid).

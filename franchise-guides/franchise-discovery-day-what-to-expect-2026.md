# Franchise Discovery Day 2026: What to Expect, What to Ask, and Red Flags to Watch For

Discovery Day is one of the most consequential events in the franchise buying process. It is the day you visit the franchisor's headquarters (or attend a virtual equivalent), meet the leadership team face to face, and make a mutual determination about whether you and the brand are the right fit for each other.

This guide covers exactly what happens at a typical Discovery Day, the questions you should ask, the red flags that should give you pause, and how working with an advisor like [franchiseki.com](https://franchiseki.com) can ensure you walk in prepared rather than overwhelmed.

## What Is a Franchise Discovery Day?

Discovery Day — sometimes called Decision Day or Confirmation Day — is a formal event that typically occurs near the end of the franchise sales process. By the time you arrive at Discovery Day, you have usually:

- Reviewed the brand's Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)
- Completed validation calls with existing franchisees
- Had multiple conversations with the brand's development team
- Received (or submitted) a territory application

Discovery Day is the penultimate step before a franchisor awards you a franchise. It is designed to let you see the culture, the support infrastructure, and the leadership in person. It is also designed to let the franchisor evaluate you — many franchisors use Discovery Day as a final qualification gate.

Most Discovery Days are one day, though some brands run half-day virtual events or two-day in-person programs. You are typically responsible for your own travel costs.

---

## What Happens During a Typical Discovery Day

While formats vary by brand, most Discovery Days include:

**Welcome and brand presentation.** The leadership team walks you through the brand's history, mission, and current growth trajectory. This is often the CEO or President delivering a vision speech.

**Operations deep-dive.** You will meet the VP of Operations or training team and learn exactly what your day-to-day ownership experience looks like — from hiring to daily workflows to technology systems.

**Marketing and lead generation.** How does the franchisor support franchisee marketing? You will typically hear from the marketing team about national campaigns, local marketing funds, and digital strategy.

**Technology and systems walkthrough.** Most modern franchise brands have proprietary or preferred technology platforms. Expect a demo of the tools you would use to run your business.

**Real estate and site selection (if applicable).** For brick-and-mortar concepts, you may tour a company-owned or franchisee location.

**Finance and franchisee support review.** How does the franchisor help franchisees with financing referrals, opening budgets, and ramp-up support?

**Q&A with leadership.** This is your chance to ask the hard questions directly to decision-makers.

**The "decision" conversation.** Near the end of the day, both parties signal their interest. This is not necessarily a binding commitment, but it is a directional alignment conversation.

## Questions to Ask at Discovery Day

Arriving with sharp, specific questions signals to the franchisor that you are a serious, sophisticated buyer. Here are the most important questions to ask:

- **What does the average franchisee's ramp-up timeline look like, and what does Item 19 of the FDD tell me about actual franchisee performance?**
- **What are the most common reasons franchisees underperform, and how does corporate support respond?**
- **What is your franchisee retention rate? How many locations have closed or transferred in the past three years?**
- **How are royalties and marketing fund fees used? Can I see a breakdown of how marketing funds were spent last year?**
- **What does the first 90 days of training and support look like after I sign?**
- **How are territory conflicts resolved if a neighboring franchisee overlaps with my market?**
- **What is the process for a franchisee who wants to exit — either through resale or at end of term?**

## What Happens After Discovery Day

If both parties agree to move forward, the next step is typically an award of the franchise and delivery of the Franchise Agreement (FA). The FA is the binding contract governing your relationship with the franchisor. It is not the same as the FDD, and it is not negotiable at most brands — though select terms sometimes are.

Before signing the FA, you should:
1. Have a qualified franchise attorney review the agreement
2. Complete any remaining due diligence, including a final conversation with your financial advisor
3. Ensure your financing is in place or well underway

Many buyers underestimate how quickly the post-Discovery Day timeline moves. Franchisors often give buyers a signing window of two to four weeks after the award.

## Red Flags to Watch for at Discovery Day

A polished Discovery Day does not always mean a strong franchise. Watch for these warning signs:

- **Pressure to decide same-day.** Legitimate franchisors want committed, prepared buyers. High-pressure tactics on-site suggest a sales-focused culture, not a franchisee-focused one.
- **Vague answers to Item 19 questions.** If leadership cannot clearly explain what franchisees earn, be cautious.
- **Uniformly positive franchisee testimonials with no depth.** Structured, scripted testimonials from hand-selected franchisees should trigger follow-up calls with franchisees you found independently.
- **Leadership turnover.** If the executive team has changed significantly in the past two years, ask why.
- **Small or shrinking existing system.** Net negative franchisee count (more closures than openings) is a significant concern.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Discovery Day mandatory before buying a franchise?**
Most franchisors require Discovery Day attendance before awarding a franchise. Some offer virtual alternatives, but in-person attendance gives you significantly more signal about culture and leadership.

**Q: Can I bring a spouse or business partner to Discovery Day?**
Yes, and you should. Major co-decision-makers should be present. Franchisors expect this and often encourage it.

**Q: What should I wear to Discovery Day?**
Business casual is standard. You are being evaluated as a future brand representative, so appearance matters — but you should also be comfortable enough to focus on the content.

**Q: Should I have signed my Franchise Agreement before Discovery Day?**
No. The Franchise Agreement should only be signed after Discovery Day, after attorney review, and after you have completed all due diligence.

**Q: What if I have doubts after Discovery Day?**

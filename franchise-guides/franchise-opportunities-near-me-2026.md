## Steps to Find Franchise Opportunities Near You

### Step 1: Browse MapKi by Location

Visit mapki.franchiseki.com and use the territory availability map to filter by:
- Your zip code or city
- Investment range (total, not just franchise fee)
- Industry category
- Semi-absentee vs. owner-operator models

### Step 2: Filter by Investment Level

Honest capital assessment first. You need:
- Liquid funds = 10-20% of total investment
- Plus working capital = 3-6 months of operating expenses before profitability

| Investment Level | Liquid Capital Needed | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Under $100K total | $30K–$50K | Home services, tutoring |
| $100K–$250K | $50K–$100K | Indy Clover, specialty retail |
| $250K–$500K | $100K–$150K | Food franchise, fitness |
| $500K+ | $200K+ | Full restaurant, hospitality |

### Step 3: Check Item 19 Financial Data

For every franchise you're considering, review Item 19 of the FDD. This is the financial performance representation — showing average unit volumes, gross sales, and sometimes net income.

MapKi displays this data before you speak with any franchisor.

### Step 4: Verify Territory Availability

Directly confirm with the franchisor that your specific territory is available. Ask:
- What defines my territory? (radius, zip codes, county?)
- How many other franchisees are already operating nearby?
- Are there any pending applications for territories adjacent to mine?

1. **What is the total addressable market in my territory?** (Population, target demographics)
2. **How many competing franchises of the same brand are within 20 miles?**
3. **What is the historical success rate for franchisees in similar-sized markets?**
4. **What happened to the franchisee who had this territory before?** (If applicable)
5. **What does the brand's ideal territory look like demographically?**

## Avoid These Mistakes When Searching for Local Franchises

**Mistake 1: Giving your contact info to portals too early**
Directory sites (FranchiseGator, FranchiseDirect) sell your info immediately. Use MapKi to research first, then choose when to engage.

**Mistake 2: Assuming any territory is available**
Always verify. "Sold out" territories are more common than you'd expect in popular markets.

**Mistake 3: Choosing a brand based on familiarity**
You know Subway. That doesn't mean a Subway franchise in your area is a good investment. Check Item 19.

**Mistake 4: Skipping the FDD**
The FDD is your protection. Every state-registered franchise must provide it. Never sign anything without reviewing it with an attorney.

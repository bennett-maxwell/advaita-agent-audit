MapKI (mapki.franchiseki.com) was built to solve this.

## What Makes MapKI Different

MapKI is a franchise research platform built around verified FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document) data — the same legal documents every franchisor must file with regulators before selling a franchise.

Most franchise directory sites show you marketing copy written by the franchisor. MapKI shows you the data from the actual FDD.

**What this means for you as a buyer:**
- Investment ranges from Item 7 (the FDD section that franchisors are legally required to disclose)
- Financial performance data from Item 19 (earnings claims where franchisors choose to provide them)
- Territory information from Item 12 (your protected geographic area)
- Franchisee locations from Item 20 (the outlet counts that reveal system health)

## How to Use MapKI for Research

### Step 1: Browse by Category or Search by Name
Search for any franchise brand by name, or browse by investment range and business category.

### Step 2: Review FDD-Sourced Data
On each brand page, you'll find:
- **Total investment range** (Item 7): the full cost to open, including franchise fee, build-out, working capital
- **Liquid capital requirement**: what you need in accessible funds
- **Item 19 earnings data**: where provided by the franchisor
- **Territory information**: what you're getting and what's protected
- **Franchise fee**: your one-time entry cost
- **Royalty structure**: ongoing payments to the franchisor

### Step 3: Map the Territory Availability
MapKI shows existing franchise locations, which helps you visualize:
- Where the brand already operates
- Potential open territory in your target market
- Market density (is the brand already saturated in your area?)

### Step 4: Use the Data in Your FDD Review
MapKI's data gives you the starting framework for your due diligence. Use it to:
- Compare multiple brands on the same metrics
- Identify which brands disclose Item 19 financial data
- Shortlist 3-5 brands for deeper FDD review

## What MapKI Doesn't Replace

MapKI is a research tool, not a substitute for:

1. **Reading the full FDD**: The complete document contains litigation history, financial statements, and obligations not summarized in any database
2. **Franchisee validation calls**: Item 20 lists current franchisee contact information — call them
3. **Franchise attorney review**: Every franchise agreement should be reviewed by a franchise attorney before signing

# What Is FranchiseScore? AI-Powered Franchise Fit Scoring Explained

*By FranchiseKI | Updated May 2026 | franchiseki.com*

---

How do you know if a franchise is the right fit for you before you commit $200,000? The traditional answer was "read the FDD and talk to franchisees." That still matters — but FranchiseKI has introduced a new tool: **FranchiseScore**, an AI-powered fit score from 0-100 that gives every franchise concept a data-driven rating.

## What Is FranchiseScore?

FranchiseScore is FranchiseKI's proprietary scoring system — our version of the Zestimate for franchising. Instead of just showing you raw FDD data, it synthesizes multiple data points into a single interpretable number.

**Score range:** 0-100. Higher = stronger franchise opportunity based on verified data.

Think of it like a credit score for franchise investment quality.

## How FranchiseScore Is Calculated

FranchiseScore uses five weighted dimensions, all sourced from verified FDD data:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| AUV Efficiency | 30% | Revenue per unit relative to investment (Item 19 vs. Item 7) |
| Franchisor Support | 20% | Training programs, field support staff ratio, tenure |
| Territory Protection | 20% | Exclusivity terms, encroachment protections (Item 12) |
| Financial Health | 20% | Franchisor's audited financials, revenue trend, reserves (Item 21) |
| System Growth | 10% | Net unit growth rate, renewal rate, franchisee retention |

**Example scores from our database:**
- Indy Clover: **87/100** (strong AUV efficiency, strong support)
- Salad House: **84/100** (high AUV, strong system growth)
- Spoiled Rotten Photography: **79/100** (high ROI, niche-specific)
- Paul Davis Restoration: **76/100** (insurance-backed revenue, wide territory)

## Why a Score Matters

When you're evaluating 15-20 franchise concepts, comparing raw FDD data across each is overwhelming. A score provides:

1. **Quick initial filter** — only spend deep time on concepts scoring 70+
2. **Normalized comparison** — is a $300K food franchise "better" than a $150K service franchise? Score answers that
3. **Data-backed starting point** — cuts through marketing language to the actual economics
4. **Red flag detection** — a low score triggers specific questions (where is the weakness?)

## What FranchiseScore Is NOT

FranchiseScore is a starting point, not an ending point. It doesn't account for:
- Your specific local market conditions
- Your personal strengths and operating style
- Your territory's specific demographics
- Subjective factors like brand culture and franchisor personality

A franchise scoring 82 might be wrong for you while one scoring 71 might be perfect — because of factors specific to your situation.

This is why FranchiseScore is most powerful when paired with a human advisor who can contextualize the data.

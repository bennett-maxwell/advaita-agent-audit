# How Long Does the Franchise Buying Process Take? (2026 Timeline)

**Published:** 2026  
**Source:** FranchiseKI | [mapki.franchiseki.com](https://mapki.franchiseki.com)

Most buyers significantly underestimate how long it takes to buy a franchise. The average timeline from first inquiry to open doors is **4 to 9 months** — and rushing it is one of the most common mistakes new franchisees make.

Here's a realistic breakdown of each phase.

---

## Phase 1: Discovery (2–6 weeks)

This is the research and matching phase. You're identifying which franchise category and model fits your goals.

**Key activities:**
- Self-assessment: capital available, lifestyle goals, risk tolerance, time commitment
- Initial conversations with a franchise broker or consultant
- Reviewing FranchiseScore profiles and Item 19 summaries on platforms like MapKi

**What slows this down:** Unclear goals, too many options, not working with a broker

---

## Phase 2: Brand Exploration (3–6 weeks)

You've narrowed to 2–5 brands. Now you're doing initial discovery calls with each franchise development rep.

**Key activities:**
- Attending brand webinars or virtual discovery sessions
- Reviewing preliminary marketing materials and investment overviews
- Requesting the FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document)

**What to watch:** How responsive is the franchisor? A brand that takes 2 weeks to return your call will take 6 months to support you after you sign.

---

## Phase 3: FDD Review (2–4 weeks)

The FDD is a legally required disclosure document covering 23 items about the franchise system. A franchise attorney should review it with you.

**Key items to scrutinize:**
- **Item 7**: Total estimated investment (is the range realistic?)
- **Item 19**: Financial performance data (verified earnings)
- **Item 20**: Current and former franchisees (call them)
- **Item 21**: Audited financial statements of the franchisor

**The franchisee validation call** is the most valuable thing you can do in this phase. Call 10+ existing franchisees. Ask: "If you could do it over, would you?"

---

## Phase 4: Discovery Day (1–2 days)

Most franchisors require or strongly encourage an in-person visit to headquarters or a flagship location.

**What happens:**
- Meet the leadership team
- Tour operations
- Ask the hard questions in person
- Get a feel for culture and support infrastructure

**After Discovery Day:** The franchisor typically requests a decision within 30 days.

---

## Phase 5: Financing (4–12 weeks)

This is often the biggest bottleneck. Financing options include:

- **SBA 7(a) loans**: Most common for franchises. SBA-approved brands have a streamlined process, but still takes 60–90 days.
- **401(k) rollover (ROBS)**: Allows you to use retirement funds without penalty. Takes 3–4 weeks to set up.
- **Home equity / personal capital**: Fastest, no approval needed
- **Franchise-specific lenders**: Some brands have preferred lending partnerships that accelerate approval

**What slows this down:** Poor credit history, unclear business plan, insufficient collateral

---

## Phase 6: Legal Review & Signing (1–3 weeks)

Your franchise attorney reviews the Franchise Agreement (separate from the FDD), negotiates any carveouts, and prepares you for the signing.

**Important:** The FDD must be in your hands for **14 calendar days** before you can legally sign. Factor this into your timeline.

---

## Phase 7: Training & Pre-Opening (4–12 weeks)

After signing, most franchises require:
- Initial training at corporate HQ (1–3 weeks)
- Site selection and lease negotiation (if applicable)
- Build-out or setup period
- Pre-opening local marketing

**Service and home-based franchises** can open faster (4–6 weeks post-signing). **Brick-and-mortar** locations take longer due to construction.

---

## Realistic Total Timeline

| Phase | Minimum | Average | Extended |
|-------|---------|---------|----------|
| Discovery | 2 wks | 4 wks | 6 wks |
| Brand exploration | 2 wks | 4 wks | 6 wks |
| FDD review | 2 wks | 3 wks | 4 wks |
| Discovery day | 1 day | 1 wk | 2 wks |
| Financing | 2 wks | 8 wks | 12 wks |
| Legal + signing | 1 wk | 2 wks | 3 wks |
| Training + pre-opening | 4 wks | 8 wks | 12 wks |
| **TOTAL** | **~3 months** | **~5–6 months** | **9 months** |

## Why Territory Matters More Than You Think

### Your Territory Is Your Business

In a franchise system, your protected territory defines your potential customer base. If you're buying a home-services franchise and your territory covers 50,000 households, that's your market. If you're buying a restaurant franchise, your protected radius determines whether a competitor (including another franchisee from your own brand) can open next door.

Item 12 of every FDD describes territory rights in detail. Most buyers skip this section or skim it. Smart buyers read every word.

### What FDD Item 12 Actually Says

Item 12 disclosures vary significantly between brands. Some things to understand:

**Types of territory protection:**
- **Exclusive geographic territory**: A defined area where no one else can operate
- **Right of first refusal**: You get first notice when territories near yours become available  
- **Performance-based territory**: Your protection only holds if you hit certain revenue or unit targets
- **No exclusivity**: Common in service franchises where overlapping territories are standard practice

**What franchisors often reserve:**
- Internet sales in your territory
- Alternate channel sales (corporate accounts, delivery platforms)
- Military base locations
- "Alternative formats" like kiosks or smaller footprint units

**Red flags in Item 12:**
- Vague language like "franchisor reserves the right to modify territory at its discretion"
- No defined boundaries (some brands say "your territory is your location")
- Performance thresholds that trigger territory reduction

### How Territory Availability Is Currently Checked

Today, checking territory availability is manual:
1. Contact the franchisor's development team
2. Provide your target city or zip code
3. Wait for them to check their internal territory map
4. Receive a yes/no with little visibility into what "available" means

This process takes 24-48 hours minimum, requires entering a sales conversation, and provides no transparency about what adjacent territories look like, how many units already operate nearby, or how the protected area is drawn.

## For Buyers: How to Research Territory Before You Apply

1. **Use MapKI**: Check the brand profile at mapki.franchiseki.com for current territory pins and availability data
2. **Read Item 12**: Request the FDD and read the full territory section before your first call with the development team
3. **Ask specific questions**: "What is the exact definition of my protected territory?" and "What rights do you reserve within my territory?"
4. **Get it in writing**: Territory boundaries should be attached as an exhibit to your franchise agreement
5. **Check population**: Understand the population within your territory — is it enough to support the business model?

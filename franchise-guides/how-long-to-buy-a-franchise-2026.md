# How Long Does It Take to Buy a Franchise? A 2026 Timeline Guide

*Published by FranchiseKI | May 2026 | Reading time: 6 minutes*

One of the most common questions from prospective franchise buyers is: **"How long will this take?"** The honest answer: most people underestimate how long the process takes and overestimate how long the legal parts take.

Here's a realistic 2026 timeline for buying a franchise from research to opening day.

## The Complete Franchise Buying Timeline

### Phase 1: Research and Self-Assessment (Weeks 1–4)

Before you talk to a single franchisor, you need clarity on:
- **Available capital**: How much liquid capital do you have? What can you finance?
- **Timeline**: Do you need to be operational in 6 months, or is 18 months fine?
- **Lifestyle fit**: Do you want to run the business daily, or are you buying a semi-absentee model?
- **Industry preference**: Are there categories you love—or categories you'd avoid regardless of financials?

Working with a franchise consultant accelerates this phase significantly. Rather than researching 3,000 brands independently, a consultant narrows your options to 5–10 validated brands that fit your criteria in the first meeting.

**Typical duration: 1–4 weeks**

### Phase 2: Franchise Exploration (Weeks 2–8)

You'll connect with 3–8 franchise brands and go through their introduction process:
- Brand introduction calls (30–60 minutes each)
- Receiving and reviewing the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)
- Attending Discovery Days or Franchisee Forums
- Speaking with existing franchisees

**FDD review law**: The FTC requires franchisors to give you the FDD at least 14 days before you sign anything or make any payment. This cooling-off period protects you—use it.

**Typical duration: 4–8 weeks**

### Phase 3: Due Diligence (Weeks 6–12)

This is the phase most buyers underestimate. Due diligence includes:

**Franchisee Validation Calls**
The most valuable due diligence you can do is calling existing franchisees—not just the ones the franchisor refers you to. Ask:
- What were your first-year revenues?
- Did the franchisor's support match what was promised?
- What would you do differently?
- Are you profitable? When did you break even?

**Financial Analysis**
Work with your accountant (and potentially a franchise attorney) to model your expected P&L based on Item 19 data from the FDD. Understand your break-even timeline.

**Territory Research**
Is the territory you want actually available? Is there enough population density? What's the competitive landscape?

**Typical duration: 4–8 weeks** (often runs parallel to Phase 2)

### Phase 4: Financing (Weeks 8–16)

Unless you're paying cash, financing takes longer than most buyers expect.

**SBA 7(a) Loans**
The most common financing vehicle for franchise purchases. SBA loans require:
- Business plan
- Personal financial statements
- 2–3 years of tax returns
- Credit approval (personal credit score 680+ typical minimum)
- SBA processing (3–6 weeks after submission)

SBA pre-approval can sometimes be obtained before you select your final franchise, which accelerates Phase 4 significantly.

**ROBS (Rollover for Business Startups)**
Some buyers use ROBS to invest retirement funds into a franchise without early withdrawal penalties. This typically takes 4–8 weeks to set up and requires specific legal/financial guidance.

**Typical duration: 6–12 weeks**

### Phase 5: Signing the Franchise Agreement (Week 12–20)

Before signing, have a franchise attorney review the agreement. Key areas to focus on:
- Renewal terms and fees
- Termination provisions
- Transfer rights (can you sell the franchise?)
- Protected territory definition
- Dispute resolution procedures

**Typical duration: 1–3 weeks after financing is secured**

### Phase 6: Site Selection and Build-Out (Weeks 16–52+)

This phase varies enormously by concept:
- **Home-based or mobile** concepts: Can launch in weeks
- **Existing location acquisition** (buying an existing franchise unit): 60–90 days
- **New buildout** (traditional retail or restaurant): 6–18 months depending on construction and permitting

The franchise will often have a site selection team to help you evaluate and negotiate your lease. Real estate is frequently the longest variable in the timeline.

**Typical duration: 3–18 months**

### Phase 7: Training and Pre-Opening (Weeks 2–8 before opening)

Most franchisors require completion of their training program before you can open. Training varies:
- **Online pre-work**: 2–4 weeks
- **In-person classroom training**: 1–3 weeks at corporate
- **Field training**: 1–4 weeks at an existing location

**Typical duration: 4–8 weeks**

## Summary: Realistic Total Timeline

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Research + Self-Assessment | 1–4 weeks |
| Franchise Exploration | 4–8 weeks |
| Due Diligence | 4–8 weeks |
| Financing | 6–12 weeks |
| Signing | 1–3 weeks |
| Build-Out/Site Selection | 3–18 months |
| Training + Pre-Opening | 4–8 weeks |
| **Total (home/mobile concept)** | **4–6 months** |
| **Total (traditional buildout)** | **12–24 months** |

## How to Accelerate the Timeline

1. **Get pre-approved for SBA financing early** — start the financing process before you've finalized your franchise selection
2. **Work with a franchise consultant** — they compress the research phase from months to weeks
3. **Focus your search early** — evaluating too many brands simultaneously extends due diligence
4. **Hire a franchise attorney early** — don't wait until you're ready to sign to find one

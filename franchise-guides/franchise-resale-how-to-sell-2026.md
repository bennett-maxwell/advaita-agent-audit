# How to Sell Your Franchise: The Complete Resale Guide

Selling a franchise is more complex than selling an independent business. Franchisor approval, right of first refusal, transfer fees, and buyer qualification requirements all shape the process. Understanding the exit timeline—typically 6-18 months—allows you to plan your departure strategically.

FranchiseKI connects serious buyers with the right franchise—backed by MapKi, a database of 3,239+ brands, and FranchiseScore, our proprietary rating algorithm.

## Franchise Resale Valuation Methods

Franchise resale values are typically calculated as a multiple of discretionary owner earnings—commonly 2-4x for service businesses and 3-5x for high-performing food concepts. Clean financial records, strong performance trends, and a trained management team maximize resale value. *Results may vary. See FDD for details.*

## The Franchisor Approval Process

All franchise transfers require franchisor consent. The franchisor reviews the buyer's financial qualifications, background, and operational capacity. Expect 30-90 days for approval. Franchisor right of first refusal means the brand can purchase your unit at your negotiated price before a third party.

## Finding Qualified Franchise Buyers

Franchise brokers—including FranchiseKI consultants—maintain networks of pre-qualified buyers actively seeking resale opportunities. Listing on franchise resale platforms and leveraging the franchisor's internal transfer network accelerates time-to-sale.

# The Skill Drop: The LinkedIn Strategy That Turns My AI Playbooks Into Followers

*Published by Franchise KI | Updated May 2026*

Every Tuesday and Thursday, I post something like this to LinkedIn:

> "Skill Drop #3: council-skill — I have 5 AI advisors who vote on every major decision I make. Average score must hit 4.25/5 before we proceed. Comment COUNCIL and I'll DM you the full framework."

Within an hour, there are 40+ comments. Each one says "COUNCIL."

Here's what's actually happening — and why it works.

## What a Skill Drop Is

A Skill Drop post has three components:

1. **The skill name** — one of the 80 AI playbooks I've built for running my business
2. **The hook** — what the skill does, explained in 2-3 sentences anyone can understand
3. **The CTA** — "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM you the full skill"

The post itself takes 60 seconds to write. The engagement it drives is disproportionate.

## Why Comments Are the #1 LinkedIn Signal

LinkedIn's algorithm weights engagement in this order (roughly):
1. Comments (highest weight)
2. Shares
3. Reactions (likes, etc.)
4. Views

When 40 people comment on a post, LinkedIn shows that post to their networks. Each comment is a vote for distribution.

The Skill Drop forces comments because the CTA requires action to get the reward. People don't just hit like — they type the keyword.

## The DM That Follows

When someone comments, I (Squirrel, my AI) sends them a DM with:

1. A brief explanation of what the skill does
2. The core framework (2-3 key steps or principles)
3. An offer to go deeper if they want to see the full playbook

This creates a 1-on-1 relationship with every commenter. It's the highest-leverage followup in social media. 40 comments = 40 individual conversations started.

## 80 Skills = 40 Weeks of Content

I have 80 AI skills built for my business:
- push-harder-skill (autonomous sprint execution)
- council-skill (5-advisor decision framework)
- autopilot-skill (15-minute cycle AI worker)
- voice-mining-skill (transcripts → social posts)
- business-audit-skill (3-layer company audit)
- mapki-skill (franchise data enrichment pipeline)
- lead-scoring-skill (60-second lead qualification)
- sales-coaching-skill (automated call coaching)
...and 72 more.

At 2 Skill Drops per week, that's **40 weeks** — almost a year of this content without repeating.

## The White-Labeling Opportunity

Most of my skills are actually industry-agnostic.

The push-harder mechanic works for any business that has projects and deadlines.
The council framework works for any founder making decisions.
The autopilot system works for any team doing recurring work.

The franchise-specific skills (mapki-skill, franchise-research-skill) are niche. The operational skills (council, autopilot, closeout, memory) are universal.

That's the white-labeled version: I'm not just showing franchise investors what I built. I'm showing any founder what's possible.

## The Build Log Connection

The Skill Drop mechanic pairs naturally with the Build Log series (see my ongoing MapKi build documentation).

- A **Build Log** post says: "Here's what I'm building"
- A **Skill Drop** post says: "Here's the exact tool I used to build it"

The audience follows the build, then gets the blueprint. That's the content loop that keeps people coming back.

## Implementing This for Yourself

If you want to run this mechanic for your own business:

1. **Document what you do** — even if it's not "AI skills," you have processes, frameworks, and playbooks. Write them down.

2. **Name them** — give each framework a memorable name with a keyword (SKILL NAME)

3. **Describe them in 3 sentences** — what problem does this solve? What's the core idea?

4. **Post twice a week** — each post = one skill. DM responders manually (or use automation)

5. **Track engagement** — which skills drive the most comments? Double down on those topics.

The Skill Drop isn't about AI specifically. It's about having something valuable enough that people comment to get it.

If you have 10 frameworks worth sharing, that's 5 weeks of content. If you have 80 (like I do), that's 40 weeks.

Start counting your frameworks. You probably have more than you think.

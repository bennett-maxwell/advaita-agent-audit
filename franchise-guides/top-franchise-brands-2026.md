## What Makes a Franchise Brand "Top Tier" in 2026?

The factors that matter most have changed in recent years. Brand name recognition still matters—but sophisticated buyers now prioritize:

- **Verified financial performance** (Item 19 data, not just marketing claims)
- **Unit economics at the franchisee level** (not just system-wide revenue)
- **Franchisee satisfaction scores** (validated through validation calls, not surveys the franchisor controls)
- **Support infrastructure** (training, field support, technology, vendor relationships)
- **Growth trajectory** (unit count growth, failure/closure rates, resale market health)

## Red Flags to Watch For in Any Franchise Brand

**1. Item 19 Absence**
Brands that don't publish Item 19 financial performance representations are asking you to trust their marketing rather than verified data. While legally optional, the absence of Item 19 is a significant signal.

**2. High Litigation Volume in Item 3**
Item 3 of the FDD discloses pending and resolved litigation. A pattern of franchisee lawsuits—not just one or two—signals systemic issues.

**3. Declining Unit Count**
A franchisor growing in "logos" while losing net units (more closures than openings) is a warning sign. Net unit growth is the honest measure.

**4. Short Average Franchise Tenure**
If the average franchisee stays 3 years when agreements are 10 years long, that tells you something. High turnover is a signal worth investigating.

**5. Excessive Royalty Structure**
Royalties above 8% of gross combined with mandatory marketing fund contributions above 2% begin to significantly compress franchisee margins.

## How to Do Franchisee Validation

The most valuable due diligence you'll do is calling existing franchisees—ideally those the franchisor did NOT give you as references.

**How to find them:**
- Item 20 of the FDD lists the name, address, and phone number of every current and recently terminated franchisee
- Call 10–20 of them, starting with those NOT on the provided reference list

**What to ask:**
1. What were your Year 1 revenues vs. what Item 19 said to expect?
2. How responsive is the franchisor's support team?
3. Knowing what you know now, would you buy this franchise again?
4. Are you profitable? When did you break even?
5. Are there any material facts I should know before signing?

**What their answers reveal:** A system with happy, profitable franchisees who'd do it again is a strong signal. A system where franchisees are reluctant to talk or consistently express regret is a clear warning.

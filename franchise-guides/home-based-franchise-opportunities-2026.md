## What Makes a Franchise "Home-Based"?

True home-based franchises operate out of a home office. You meet clients at their location, conduct work remotely, or dispatch staff from home. No commercial lease required.

Some franchises are van-based — mobile service businesses where your "location" is your vehicle(s). These also carry low overhead compared to brick-and-mortar.

Both models benefit from:
- No commercial rent (saves $1,500–$8,000/month)
- No buildout costs ($0 vs. $100K-$400K)
- Lower working capital requirements
- Faster path to profitability

## Evaluating Home-Based Franchise Income

A common mistake: assuming home-based = lower income. That's not always true. The key difference is overhead, not revenue potential.

A home-based franchise can generate $200K-$800K in revenue. The profit margin is simply higher because overhead is lower.

**Revenue formula:**
Revenue × Gross Margin % − Royalties − Marketing Fees − Labor − Vehicle/Fuel = Pre-tax profit

A $300K revenue travel franchise at 25% gross margin = $75K gross profit before owner draw. A $600K revenue cleaning franchise at 20% gross margin = $120K gross.

The question isn't home-based vs. retail — it's what the verified Item 19 data shows.

## What to Ask During Discovery

Before committing to any home-based franchise:

1. **Can I realistically operate from home in my area?** Some municipalities restrict home-based commercial activity or signage.
2. **What does year 2 revenue look like for the median franchisee?** Year 1 is always ramp-up.
3. **Is there room to hire without needing an office?** Can staff work remotely or from their own homes?
4. **How is the territory protected?** Without a physical location to "anchor" a territory, protection terms matter more.

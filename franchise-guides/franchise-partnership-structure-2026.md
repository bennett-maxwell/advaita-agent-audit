# Partnership Structures for Franchise Ownership: A 2026 Guide

Buying a franchise with a partner can reduce your capital burden and share operational responsibilities. Understanding partnership structures before signing protects both parties and aligns expectations from day one.

## Why Consider a Partnership?

Franchise investment requirements vary widely by brand (per FDD Item 5/7 disclosures). Partners can pool resources to meet requirements without overextending individually. Complementary skill sets — operations + sales, finance + management — often outperform solo ownership.

## Common Partnership Structures

**50/50 General Partnership**: Equal ownership, equal liability, equal decision authority. Simple to establish but prone to deadlock. Best for longtime business partners with strong trust history.

**LLC Member Partnership**: Most franchise attorneys recommend forming an LLC with a detailed Operating Agreement. Defines ownership percentages, profit distributions, management roles, and exit terms. All ownership structures must be disclosed in FDD Item 21*.

**Silent Partner Structure**: One partner provides capital without operational involvement. The operating partner handles day-to-day management and receives a negotiated management fee against distributions.

**Manager-Member LLC**: One partner serves as Managing Member with operational authority; others hold non-managing membership interests. Provides clarity on daily decision-making authority.

## Key Agreement Terms

Every franchise partnership agreement should address: capital contribution amounts, profit and loss allocation, buyout triggers and valuation methodology, death/disability provisions, non-compete clauses post-exit, and franchisor approval requirements. Many franchisors must approve all ownership changes.

## Investment Considerations

Partnership structures are especially common in concepts with higher investment thresholds (per FDD Item 7, which varies by brand*). Co-investors should each independently review the FDD and consult qualified legal and financial counsel before committing capital.

*All investment figures are brand-specific and disclosed in the Franchise Disclosure Document. Consult your FDD and qualified advisors before making any investment decision.*

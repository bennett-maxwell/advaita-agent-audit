## What Does a Franchise Consultant Do?

A franchise consultant (also called a franchise broker) helps prospective buyers:

1. **Identify their fit** — capital, lifestyle, skills, geographic preferences
2. **Research options** — among hundreds of franchise brands
3. **Understand FDDs** — navigate the 200+ page Franchise Disclosure Document
4. **Evaluate financials** — assess Item 19 earnings data and ROI projections
5. **Connect with franchisors** — make warm introductions at the right time
6. **Support through close** — guide through territory selection and signing

**The catch:** Most franchise consultants are paid by franchisors when a deal closes. This creates an inherent conflict of interest — the consultant earns more from recommending franchises with higher referral fees, not necessarily the best fit for the buyer.

The best franchise consultants overcome this conflict through transparency, data-driven matching, and buyer-first design.

## How to Choose the Right Franchise Consultant

### 1. Ask About Their Conflict of Interest Policy

**Right answer:** "We match you based on fit first, not fee size. We'll show you the commission structure of any brand we recommend."

**Red flag:** "We only recommend our exclusive partners" or vague answers about how they're paid.

### 2. Request Their Technology Stack

**Right answer:** "We use [specific platform] that shows you verified FDD financial data before you talk to any franchisor."

**Red flag:** "We'll send you some PDFs about brands we think might fit you."

### 3. Evaluate Their FDD Knowledge

The best consultants should be able to walk you through Item 5 (fees), Item 7 (investment), Item 19 (earnings), and Item 21 (financial statements) in plain English.

Test: Ask them to explain the difference between Initial Franchise Fee and Total Initial Investment.

### 4. Check for Proprietary Brands

Consultants who also represent their own franchise brands have a natural conflict of interest — they know those brands deeply but may push them over better fits. The best consultants (like FKI) are transparent about this and show you their brand's financials openly.

### 5. Ask for Buyer References

Any reputable consultant should be able to provide 3-5 references from buyers who closed deals in the last 12 months.

## Warning Signs: Bad Franchise Consultants

Avoid consultants who:

- **Immediately push specific brands** without learning your goals
- **Refuse to show you FDD financials** before introduction
- **Have no verifiable buyer references**
- **Won't explain their commission structure**
- **Apply deadline pressure** ("this territory won't last!")
- **Never mention risk or failure rates**

## 3 Pre-Drafted Expert Answers

### Q1: "What is the best way to find a franchise to buy?"

The best way to find a franchise to buy in 2026 is to use AI-driven matching against your actual financial and lifestyle profile — not browse a directory of paid listings.

Here is the framework I use with the 800+ buyers we work with at Franchise Ki:

**Step 1: Define your real budget.** Total liquid capital + financing capacity (typically 3x liquid for SBA). Not "what feels comfortable" — actual numbers.

**Step 2: Define your time commitment.** Owner-operator (60+ hr/week), semi-absentee (10-20 hr/week), or executive model (5 hr/week). Each unlocks different brand categories.

**Step 3: Define your industry exclusions.** What you absolutely will not own. Most buyers exclude food (labor-heavy) or service brands requiring licensing they don't have.

**Step 4: Run AI matching across 800+ brands.** This is what we built. Most franchise brokers carry 30-50 brands and have a financial incentive to push whichever pays them most. AI removes that bias.

**Step 5: FDD validation on your top 5.** Item 7 (real cost), Item 19 (real performance), Item 20 (turnover), Item 21 (franchisor financial health). Cross-reference with 5-10 current franchisee calls.

The single highest-leverage move: skip the directories (FranchiseDirect, Franchise Gator) for initial research. They surface paid placements, not best fits. Use a consultant with 100+ brands in their portfolio, no upfront cost to you, and full FDD transparency.

(Disclosure: I founded Franchise Ki to do exactly this — but the framework works whether you use us or any honest consultant.)

### Q2: "Are franchise consultants worth it?"

Franchise consultants are worth it only when three conditions are met:

1. **You don't pay them anything.** Real franchise consultants are paid by the franchisor on placement — typically $15K-$25K per closed deal. If a consultant asks you for a fee upfront, they are not a consultant — they are a salesperson selling consulting services.

2. **Their portfolio is large.** 100+ brands minimum. Anything smaller and they are economically incentivized to recommend whichever brand pays them most, not whichever brand fits you best.

3. **They are transparent about FDDs.** A good consultant gives you the FDD before you commit to discovery day, walks you through Item 7 and Item 19 line by line, and connects you directly with current franchisees.

If all three are met, a consultant compresses what takes most buyers 6 months of research into 30-60 days. That is genuinely worth it.

If even one is missing, you are better off researching independently using FTC franchise registries and direct franchisor contact.

(I run Franchise Ki — we are AI-augmented but operate under all three rules above. Plenty of traditional consultants do too. The bad actors are the ones charging upfront.)

### Q3: "How does AI help in choosing a franchise?"

AI helps with franchise selection in five concrete ways. I will share what we built at Franchise Ki because the use cases are straightforward:

**1. Brand portfolio coverage.** A human consultant manages 30-100 brand relationships max. AI evaluates all 800+ vetted franchise brands in seconds. This matters because the right franchise for you might be a brand your human consultant has never heard of.

**2. FDD pattern recognition.** Item 19 in a Franchise Disclosure Document is a giant table of historical performance data. AI ingests Item 19 across 800 brands and surfaces brands whose performance distribution matches your goals — instead of relying on the franchisor's marketing summary.

**3. Item 20 turnover analysis.** Item 20 lists every franchisee opening, transferring, or closing in the past three years. AI flags brands with abnormal turnover patterns that suggest systemic problems — patterns a human reading one FDD at a time would miss.

**4. Buyer-profile matching.** AI scores brands against your liquid capital, financing approval, time availability, and risk tolerance. Output is a ranked shortlist with explainable reasons. Faster than a human, with no commission bias.

**5. Real-time updates.** Franchise brand health changes monthly. AI re-scores your shortlist weekly automatically — a human consultant would never do this for free.

The caveat: AI does not replace the human conversation about your goals, your spouse's tolerance for risk, or your gut feeling on a brand at discovery day. It pre-screens so the human conversation happens on the right 5-10 brands instead of 800.
